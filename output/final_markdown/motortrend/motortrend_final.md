## The 2027 Mercedes-Benz EQS Fixes Its Biggest Problems—But Is It Enough?

Source URL: <https://www.motortrend.com/news/first-look-2027-mercedes-benz-eqs-class>

- Mercedes is reworking the 2027 EQS with a serious “running-gear lift” after the original EQS-Class sedan’s 400-volt architecture hurt charging speed; the facelift also aims to move past the model’s 0.20 drag-coefficient “soap-bar” styling and tepid late-2024 refresh.
- The V297 EQS still rides on its original MEA EV architecture, but borrows MB.EA tech: newer/more efficient drive units, a rear motor with a two-speed transaxle, 11:1 torque multiplication at launch, then 5:1 gearing at roughly any speed up to 70-ish mph.
- That two-speed setup creates an added peak-efficiency operating zone and helps the rear motor hold peak power to the 130-mph top speed.
- Battery upgrades include new silicon-oxide/graphite anodes in a lower-cobalt chemistry, raising capacity to 122 kWh in the same package space that formerly held 118 kWh.
- Charging and regen improve too: the EQS battery can split into two 400-volt packs for legacy Tesla chargers, enabling up to 350 kW on the best charging towers, 175 kW on 400-volt equipment, and braking-energy recuperation at up to 385 kW.

## Bosch’s Wild New Way to Clean Up Gas Engines Starts With a Flame

Source URL: <https://www.motortrend.com/news/bosch-rapid-catalyst-heater-gas-burner>

- Bosch’s Rapid Catalyst Heater targets the dirtiest cold-start seconds of a drive, when criteria emissions still spike even as CO2 rules are being rolled back; the tech is aimed at cleaner gas cars and potentially much cleaner PHEVs.
- Modern three-way catalytic converters can cut up to 98% of criteria emissions once warmed to 750–1,100 degrees F, so emissions certification hinges on the first 20–60 seconds of the EPA’s 31-minute dyno test.
- Bosch says the heater’s gas-burner approach can deliver almost instantly 25 kW of heat directly into the exhaust stream ahead of the catalyst, versus 1–10 kW for older direct electric catalyst heaters (5 kW typical), a load that is hard to support on a 12-volt system without a hybrid battery.
- The system uses a secondary air-injection-type pump, filtered air through a Bosch mass airflow sensor, low-pressure fuel via a standard Bosch port injector with a unique nozzle-hole pattern, ignition by a Bosch diesel glow plug, and a Bosch oxygen sensor targeting 14.7:1 air-fuel ratio.
- The burner then feeds the heated mixture into the exhaust right at the catalyst entrance.

## Aramco Built a Hybrid to Beat Toyota—And It’s Wildly Different

Source URL: <https://www.motortrend.com/news/aramco-dedicated-hybrid-engine-hev-phev-powertrain>

- Aramco—the Saudi Arabian Oil Company and the world’s largest oil producer—has built a Dedicated Hybrid Engine (DHE) to keep combustion relevant as hybrids out-sell battery-electric vehicles in some regions.
- The effort sits alongside Horse Powertrain, the Renault/Geely-Volvo combustion JV in which Aramco owns 10% (with the other two holding 45% each); Horse makes turnkey engines and range-extenders for quick hybridization, while Aramco’s DHE is a clean-sheet “ideal new hybrid powertrain from scratch.”
- Aramco’s concept echoes Toyota-style planetary hybrid drive, but uses a motor-generator-planetary setup at each end of the crankshaft and is tuned for a strong-hybrid duty cycle that spends long periods in an “efficiency island.”
- The proof-of-concept engine is a 1,599cc inline three-cylinder cam-in-block pushrod design with a monoblock casting, no separate cylinder head, two valves per cylinder, no variable timing or lift, and a push for compactness and simplicity.
- To cut friction, the engine uses roller bearings for the crank, camshaft, and connecting rods, offsets the crank 12 mm from the cylinder-bore centers to reduce side loading, and deletes the accessory drive so auxiliaries including the water pump run electrically on demand.

## What’s Going on at Ford? A Shakeup That Could Change Everything

Source URL: <https://www.motortrend.com/news/ford-restructuring-ev-software-defined-vehicles-2026>

- Ford announced a major April 15, 2026 restructuring into a new Product Creation and Industrialization organization to accelerate vehicle development and improve efficiency as it pivots toward EVs and software-defined vehicles (SDVs).
- The new unit combines product development, design, engineering, purchasing, and manufacturing into one lifecycle organization intended to streamline decisions, cut costs, and shorten timelines.
- COO Kumar Galhotra will lead the group, with Alan Clarke keying advanced vehicle engineering and the rollout of Ford’s Universal Electric Vehicle Platform (UEV); Galhotra has previously led Ford Blue, Lincoln, marketing, and engineering.
- Doug Field—Ford’s former chief EV, digital and design officer, who joined in 2022 after Tesla and Apple—is departing as the company moves to a more unified operating model; he said Ford’s technology strategy is “winning,” the first breakthrough product off the Universal EV platform is a mid-size pickup, and core technologies are “near the top of the industry.”
- Ford says the reorg supports a bigger product cadence: refresh 80% of its North American portfolio and 70% of its global portfolio by 2029, starting with a new small electric pickup built on UEV and using in-house tech including what it bills as the world’s cheapest electric motors.
