## Stellantis and Microsoft sign five-year AI deal | Automotive World

Source URL: <https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/stellantis-and-microsoft-sign-five-year-ai-deal/>

- Stellantis and Microsoft have signed a five-year strategic collaboration to co-develop AI, cybersecurity and cloud capabilities across Stellantis’ global operations.
- The companies will run more than 100 AI initiatives across customer care, product development and operations, including AI-powered validation, predictive maintenance and faster digital-service deployment.
- Stellantis will also deploy an AI-driven global cyber defence centre covering IT systems, connected vehicles, manufacturing sites and digital products, aimed at faster threat detection and consistent protection of services and customer data.
- Microsoft Azure modernisation targets a 60% reduction in Stellantis’ data centre footprint by 2029, alongside 20,000 Microsoft 365 Copilot licences and a dedicated training programme.

## AECC proposes data-first framework for vehicle data | Automotive World

Source URL: <https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/aecc-proposes-data-first-framework-for-vehicle-data/>

- The Automotive Edge Computing Consortium (AECC) has published a white paper proposing a distributed, data-first architecture for the rising data loads from connected and software-defined vehicles.
- AECC says modern vehicles can generate tens of gigabytes of data per day, beyond traditional IoT framework capacity; the proposed model uses three layers: peer-to-peer local exchange/aggregation, an edge layer using localised resources such as edge data centres and Wi-Fi access points, and a mobile/cloud layer for central coordination, large-scale data management and long-term storage.
- The consortium says handling data at the most appropriate layer improves energy efficiency and reduces network strain.
- Dr Ryokichi Onishi, AECC Board Chairperson, said the architecture combines cellular, Wi-Fi and inter-vehicle transfer with distributed computing across vehicles, edge infrastructure and cloud platforms to manage automotive data at scale; AECC says it will also support AI uses beyond driving, including infotainment, voice assistants and personalised travel recommendations.

Source: [AECC](https://aecc.org/aecc-introduces-data-first-architecture-to-advance-scalable-data-driven-automotive-services/)
