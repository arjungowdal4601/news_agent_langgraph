American Honda is recalling approximately 440,000 model year 2018 to 2022 Odyssey vehicles in the US to address an airbag software calibration issue. The automaker said the supplemental restraint system (SRS) electronic control unit in affected vehicles is too sensitive to underbody impacts, which could cause side curtain or side airbags to deploy unexpectedly.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/16082614/honda-odyssey-2018-2022.jpg)


Honda said strong impacts from potholes, speed bumps or road debris could trigger unintended deployment, increasing the risk of a crash or injury. As of 2 April 2026, the company had received 25 reports of injuries and no reports of fatalities linked to the issue, covering a period from January 2017 to April 2026.

Authorised Honda dealers will reprogram or, where necessary, replace the SRS ECU with updated airbag deployment parameters. Registered owners of affected vehicles will be notified by post and asked to visit a dealer for the repair.
