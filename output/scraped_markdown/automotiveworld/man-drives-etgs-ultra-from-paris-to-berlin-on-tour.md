MAN is sending its eTGS Ultra semitrailer tractor on a 1,000 km journey from Paris to Berlin as part of the Milence Electric Tour, running from 15 to 23 April. The tour aims to demonstrate the technical and economic viability of electric long-distance freight transport across Europe.

Milence, a charging infrastructure joint venture between TRATON GROUP, Daimler Truck and Volvo Group, is organising the tour. The route passes through charging parks in France, Belgium, the Netherlands and Germany.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/16075312/man-etgs-milence-1024x683.jpg)


The eTGS Ultra and its eTGX Ultra counterpart are the only standard lowliner electric trucks on the market with a maximum battery capacity of 534 kW/h, enabling a range of up to 570 km without intermediate charging. MAN said the vehicles’ short wheelbase and low semi-trailer height make both models suited to high-volume automotive trailers.

In a statement, Friedrich Baumann, Member of the Executive Board for Sales and Customer Solutions at MAN Truck & Bus, said: The vehicles and services are ready, and now there is a need for an increased expansion of the public charging infrastructure throughout Europe. The transition to electric mobility in road freight transport can only succeed if we work together.

MAN also highlighted its Charge&Go service, which provides truck-compatible public charging access across Europe. The truckmaker’s broader offering spans route planning, energy monitoring, financing and driver training.
