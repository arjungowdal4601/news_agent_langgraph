Silicon Box has joined imec’s Automotive Chiplet Program, a research consortium of more than 22 partners spanning vehicle manufacturers, Tier 1 suppliers, and semiconductor companies. The Singapore-based advanced packaging firm will contribute expertise in chiplet interconnection architecture, package development, and testing to support automotive-grade chiplet standards.

Chiplet technology—which combines multiple smaller dies in a single package rather than relying on a single large chip—is increasingly seen as a way to manage rising compute requirements in software-defined vehicles while controlling costs and supply chain risk. Silicon Box is already co-developing chiplet-based packages with customers for next-generation ADAS applications and received an IATF 16949 Letter of Conformance last month ahead of full automotive certification.

The company reported that revenue from automotive customers grew at an annualised rate of 436% in the first quarter of 2026. Silicon Box hit a 100-million-unit production milestone at its Singapore foundry last October and is planning a second facility in Novara, Italy, targeting a 2028 production start.

“Chiplet technology is gaining traction within the automotive chip industry for its potential to shorten time-to-market, ensure stable supply and reduce development costs,” said co-founder and Chief Executive BJ Han. Imec vice-president automotive Bart Plackle said Silicon Box’s involvement would “accelerate progress and mitigate risks in the transition towards a fully interoperable chiplet-based ecosystem.”
