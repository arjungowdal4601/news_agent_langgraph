Toyota Motor Europe has opened a new Toyota Digital Hub in Wrocław, Poland, where around 200 software specialists will develop connected vehicle applications, cloud infrastructure and cybersecurity solutions for Toyota and Lexus models across Europe. The hub marks a further expansion of Toyota’s presence in the city, which has hosted a Shared Services Centre since 2015.

The Wrocław team will work on the MyToyota and LexusLink+ applications, used by more than two million European customers to access remote vehicle functions, battery charge monitoring and ownership services. Work will also cover the cloud infrastructure underpinning connected services for both brands across the region.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/14173534/toyota-digital-hub-building-wroclaw-poland-1024x750.jpg)


Luis Lopes, Vice President, Information and Technology, Toyota Motor Europe, said: “The Toyota Digital Hub is set to play an important role in Europe by expanding Toyota’s software development capabilities and positioning Toyota Motor Europe as a trusted partner in future global software-defined vehicle development.”

Thierry Boitel, Vice President, Research and Development, Toyota Motor Europe, said: “Poland was selected as the location for the Toyota Digital Hub due to its strong pool of highly skilled specialists and the presence of leading technical universities.”
