Motive Companies has deployed a GXC private cellular network, designed to facilitate the deployment of autonomous vehicles, for Raymond West in Cypress, California. The network supports the Toyota Automated Logistics ML2 Mini Load, which transports parts from stockroom to shop floor across the full length of the facility.

Motive conducted an RF propagation study before designing the deployment to address coverage challenges posed by high-bay racking, steel structures, and heavy equipment throughout the site. The network is monitored around the clock through Motive’s Network Operations Centre, providing proactive diagnostics and operational visibility.

Raymond West selected the ML2 and the GXC network as part of a broader automation expansion. The private cellular infrastructure is designed to scale alongside additional automated guided vehicles and autonomous mobile robots as the company grows its automation footprint.

Motive already provides solar, battery energy storage, and EV charging at the Raymond West facility. The GXC deployment extends that to connectivity, with the company positioning itself as a single integrated infrastructure partner across the site.
