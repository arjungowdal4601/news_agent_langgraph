Volkswagen has revealed the ID.3 Neo, a comprehensively updated successor to the ID.3 electric compact model, with pre-sales starting on 16 April 2026 in Germany and across European markets. The vehicle features a new exterior based on the automaker’s Pure Positive design language, a redesigned interior and a more efficient drive system offering up to 630 km of WLTP range.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/16081528/volkswagen-id-3-neo-1-1024x683.jpg)


The ID.3 Neo will be available in three trim levels and with three powertrain configurations. Outputs range from 125 kW to 170 kW, paired with battery capacities of 50 kW/h, 58 kW/h and 79 kW/h net. The largest battery supports DC charging at up to 183 kW, while the two smaller packs charge at up to 105 kW.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/16081538/volkswagen-id-3-neo-2-1024x683.jpg)


Inside, Volkswagen has fitted a new Innovision infotainment system with a 12.9-inch central display and 10.25-inch digital cockpit. The automaker said the cabin features redesigned controls, a new multifunction steering wheel and higher-quality materials. Optional technologies include Connected Travel Assist with traffic light recognition, one-pedal driving and a vehicle-to-load function capable of supplying up to 3.6 kW to external devices.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/16081548/volkswagen-id-3-neo-3-1024x683.jpg)


Martin Sander, Member of the Board of Management for Sales, Marketing and After Sales, said: “In addition to the long range, models such as the ID.3 Neo impress with a superior, timeless design that provides long-lasting pleasure, high quality for a noticeably first-class driving experience and technical perfection that can always be relied on.”
