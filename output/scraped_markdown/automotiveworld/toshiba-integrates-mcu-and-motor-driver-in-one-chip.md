Toshiba Electronic Devices & Storage Corporation has begun shipping engineering samples of the TB9M030FG, a new addition to its SmartMCD series of automotive motor control devices. The chip integrates a microcontroller (MCU) and motor driver into a single 9×9 mm package, targeting applications such as electric water pumps, oil pumps, fans and blowers.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/16082642/toshiba-smart-motor-control-driver-TB9M030FG.jpg)


The device incorporates Toshiba’s proprietary sensorless control technology, which enables field oriented control (FOC) of three-phase brushless DC motors from zero speed without position sensors. Toshiba said this approach eliminates the noise associated with conventional high-frequency signal injection methods, resulting in quieter motor operation.

Built around an Arm Cortex-M0 core, the TB9M030FG includes flash memory, a gate driver for N-channel power MOSFETs, a local interconnect network transceiver, and a power system capable of operating at automotive battery voltage levels. Toshiba said the integration contributes to electronic control unit miniaturisation and lower component counts as automakers seek to reduce board space. The device is qualified to the AEC-Q100 Grade 0 automotive standard, with mass production scheduled for January 2027.
