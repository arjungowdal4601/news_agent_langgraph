Massimo Group has entered into a preliminary cooperation agreement with Shenzhen AIBO Robotics, a Chinese intelligent service robot developer. The deal is a framework only, with pricing, deployment plans, and revenue-sharing structures subject to further negotiation — and no assurance that any specific order or revenue will result.

Under the agreement, MAMO intends to explore the introduction and localisation of AIBO’s robotic systems across commercial and retail environments in the US and China, drawing on its US-based assembly capabilities. The company also plans to evaluate AI and automation upgrades to its existing golf cart and utility vehicle platforms, potentially covering semi-autonomous navigation, obstacle detection, and remote monitoring functions.

“By combining AIBO’s robotics technology with our strengths in commercialisation, deployment execution, electromechanical integration, US-based assembly capabilities, and existing vehicle platform experience, we believe MAMO is positioned to evaluate practical automation applications across a range of real-world environments,” said Chief Executive David Shan.
