GAC’s AION UT electric hatchback recorded over 2,400 deliveries in Thailand and more than 700 in Mexico in the first quarter, following its 2025 global debut. Indonesia saw deliveries exceed 800 units, while Hong Kong SAR accumulated over 1,000 orders. Australia generated 600 pre-orders within the model’s first month on sale.

Smaller volumes were recorded in Uruguay, Colombia, and Singapore. The model has received segment awards at the Bangkok International Motor Show, Mexico’s Top 26 Auto Show, and the Indonesia International Auto Show, along with recognition at Thailand’s Car of the Year 2026 event. The model is also in the process of launching across multiple key European markets through 2026.

The AION UT is built on a 2,750mm wheelbase and supports 24-minute fast charging and vehicle-to-load functionality. GAC says its Magazine Battery 2.0 system has recorded zero thermal runaway incidents. The model was styled at GAC’s Milan Design Centre.
