Mahle reported full-year 2025 revenue of €11.3bn, down from prior years, as currency depreciation and the disposal of joint venture stakes and product divisions reduced reported sales. Stripping out those effects, organic growth was 0.6%. The group posted a net profit for the year, extending an operational turnaround it first achieved in 2023.

Adjusted Ebit rose to €442m, with the adjusted margin improving to 3.9%. Net debt fell and the equity ratio increased. The results were achieved alongside significant restructuring provisions as the company continued to reduce headcount and consolidate its production network in response to weakening demand in Europe and North America.

The Thermal and Fluid Systems unit, Mahle’s largest, held sales at €6.1bn on an organic basis. Powertrain and Charging grew 1.9% in organic terms to €3.9bn, though the company cited a slow EV ramp-up in Europe as a drag. The Lifecycle and Mobility aftermarket unit generated €1.2bn, up 1.7% organically.

“We are rigorously pursuing our efficiency programme ‘Back on Track’,” said Chairman and Chief Executive Arnd Franz, citing geopolitical uncertainty, energy costs, exchange rate volatility, and Chinese competition as factors that made 2025 “another challenging business year.”
