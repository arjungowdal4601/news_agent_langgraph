Rivian and Redwood Materials have announced a partnership to deploy battery energy storage at Rivian’s manufacturing facility in Normal, Illinois. Using more than 100 second-life Rivian battery packs, the system will initially provide 10MWh of dispatchable energy to reduce costs and ease grid load during peak demand periods.

Rivian will supply the battery packs to Redwood, which will integrate them into a storage system supported by its Redwood Pack Manager technology. The stored energy will be available for on-site use at the Normal plant, with the system designed to be rapidly scalable.

“EVs represent a massive, distributed and highly competitive energy resource,” said Rivian Chief Executive RJ Scaringe. “Our partnership with Redwood enables us to utilise our vehicle’s batteries beyond the life of a vehicle and contribute to grid health and American competitiveness.”

Redwood Materials founder and Chief Executive JB Straubel said electricity demand was outpacing grid expansion, adding that the partnership demonstrated how EV battery packs could be brought online quickly as dispatchable energy resources “without waiting years for new infrastructure.”
