Glydways has raised US$170m in an oversubscribed Series C co-led by Suzuki Motor Corporation, ACS Group, and Khosla Ventures. Existing investors Mitsui Chemicals and Gates Frontier participated alongside new backer Obayashi Corporation, bringing Glydways’ total funding to over US$250m.

The round will fund three operational pilots launching in 2026 in the UAE, the greater New York area, and South Metro Atlanta, where the company has already broken ground on its first publicly accessible system. Large-scale operations are targeted from 2027. Glydways Chief Executive Mark Seeger [previously hinted](https://www.automotiveworld.com/articles/should-autonomous-transit-take-a-disaggregated-approach/) to *Automotive World *that Japan is among the locations it is in talks to deploy its technology in; investments from Japanese firms would appear to support this.

Glydways’ approach eschews the traditional approach to autonomous driving which uses existing road infrastructure, instead pairing its in-house-developed vehicles with dedicated small guideways to create on-demand networks it says deliver ten times the throughput and cost efficiency of existing urban transit.

“Glydways will be in public operations this year, showing the public a new way of moving around cities that is efficient and affordable,” said Mark Seeger, founder and co-Chief Executive. Suzuki Motor Corporation President Toshihiro Suzuki said the company’s mission aligned with Suzuki’s “By Your Side” corporate ethos, adding that the investment would “strengthen this synergy” as both parties advance toward “the mobility of the future.”
