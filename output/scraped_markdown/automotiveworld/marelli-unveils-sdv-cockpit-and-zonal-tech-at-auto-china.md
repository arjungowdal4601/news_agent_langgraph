Marelli will showcase a range of in-cabin technologies at Auto China 2026 in Beijing from 24 April, including a unified software-defined cockpit platform, zonal electrical architecture components and a 5G RedCap connectivity module. The supplier said the technologies are designed to support brand differentiation as vehicle standardisation increases across the industry.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/10173520/Marelli_HorizonView-2-1024x398.jpg)


The cockpit platform combines computing, artificial intelligence (AI), graphics and human-machine interface (HMI) functions in a single system built on a QNX real-time operating system architecture. Display technologies on show include an ultrathin MiniLED full array local dimming (FALD) screen and a 44.8-inch HorizonView pillar-to-pillar display capable of up to 12,000 nits brightness. On the architecture side, microcontroller-free door and seat modules shift window, mirror and seat functions including anti-pinch protection to a zone control unit, reducing electronic control unit (ECU) count and wiring complexity.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/10173443/Marelli_Affordable-5G-RedCap-1024x682.jpg)


Marelli’s 5G reduced capability (RedCap) telematics module delivers 50% higher data rates and 2.5 times lower latency than 4G at comparable cost, supporting over-the-air updates, remote diagnostics and dual-frequency satellite positioning.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/10173557/marelli-mcu-free-modules-1024x576.jpg)


In a statement, Joachim Fetzer, Chief Technology and Innovation Officer at Marelli, said: “At Auto China, we will demonstrate how Marelli unifies compute, display, audio, and interior design into a coherent, software-defined experience enabled by deep local engineering and strong partnerships.”
