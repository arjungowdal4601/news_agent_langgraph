The share of renewable energy in EU road and rail transport fell from 7.5% in 2023 to 7.4% in 2024 when measured without artificial multipliers, according to new Eurostat data analysed by ePURE, the European renewable ethanol association. Fossil fuels still accounted for 92.1% of transport energy in real terms.

Crop-based biofuels including renewable ethanol remain the largest source of renewable energy in EU transport, making up 43.6% of the renewables mix. However, their use is capped under the Renewable Energy Directive at each member state’s 2020 share plus one percentage point, to a maximum of 7%. Only four member states, Sweden, the Netherlands, Finland and Austria, exceeded 10% renewable energy in transport in 2024 without multipliers. Both Sweden and Finland recorded significant declines compared with 2023.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/16195515/epure-fossil-fuels-dependence-2024.jpg)


David Carpintero, Director General of ePURE, said: “Every year that EU policies unfairly restrain member states from using sustainable crop-based biofuels to replace fossil fuels is another year lost in the fight for EU energy independence and against climate change.”

ePURE argued that with conflict in the Middle East increasing the cost of Europe’s dependence on imported fossil fuel, there is an urgent need to support renewables that reduce transport emissions, including European renewable ethanol.
