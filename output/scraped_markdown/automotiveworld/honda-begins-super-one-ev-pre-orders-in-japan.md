Honda Motor will begin accepting pre-orders for its Super-ONE compact electric vehicle (EV) in Japan on 16 April 2026, ahead of a late-May market launch. The A-segment model weighs 1,090 kg and offers a 274 km range under worldwide harmonised light vehicle test conditions, placing it among the lightest passenger EVs in the Japanese market.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/10090144/honda-sper-one-1024x683.jpg)


The Super-ONE is built on an adapted version of Honda’s N Series lightweight platform, with a widened tread and a thin battery mounted under the floor to lower the centre of gravity. A dedicated BOOST Mode increases maximum power output from 47 kW to 70 kW, synchronising with a simulated seven-speed transmission and an Active Sound Control system to replicate gearshift feel and engine sound from conventional vehicles.

Five exterior colours are available, including Boost Violet Pearl, a deep purple shade developed for the Super-ONE. An eight-speaker Bose Premium Sound System is fitted as standard, making the Super-ONE the first Honda compact model in Japan to feature Bose audio.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/10090154/honda-sper-one-2-1024x575.jpg)


The vehicle will be displayed at Honda’s stand at the Automobile Council 2026 at Makuhari Messe from 10 to 12 April, with pre-orders opening on 16 April at Honda Cars dealerships nationwide.
