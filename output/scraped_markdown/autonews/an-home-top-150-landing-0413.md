### Vehicle sales* by top 10 groups

The top 10 groups on this year's top 150 list sold more than a 10th of the U.S. industry's combined retail and fleet new-vehicle sales in 2025. Their 1.8 million new-vehicle sales represented an 11.1 percent share, up 0.3 points from the percentage controlled by the top 10 in 2024.

### Vehicle sales* by top 150 groups

This year’s top 150 dealership groups continued the trend of top 150 dealers collectively controlling a greater amount of the new-vehicle market each year since 2019. The top 150 in 2025 made 27 percent of the U.S. industry’s combined retail and fleet new-vehicle sales, up 0.3 points from a year earlier.

### Store count drops for top 10 groups

The top 10 dealership groups collectively had 1,885 dealerships in 2025, 10.3 percent of all U.S. dealerships. This count and share was down slightly from the top 10 for 2024.

### Top 150 dealership share climbs

The top 150 dealership groups in 2025 sold a greater percentage of the U.S. industry’s new vehicles than their 2024 counterparts, despite having slightly fewer stores.
