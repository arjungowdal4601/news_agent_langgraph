# 2027 Porsche 911 GT3 S/C First Look: Channeling the Speedster’s Open Top Spirit

No roof, no back seats, no automatic? Yes, please.

[Alex Leanse](https://www.motortrend.com/staff/alex-leanse)WriterManufacturerPhotographer

Over Porsche’s history, [the 911 lineup](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/porsche/911) has contained what seems like several billion variants of what’s fundamentally the same car. Even within that multitude of models, though, there’s never exactly been a 911 GT3 convertible—until now.

The 2027 Porsche 911 GT3 S/C offers what was once exclusive to limited-run, invite-only-type cars. This so-called Sport Cabriolet is a parts-bin special in the best way, mixing bits from several of the most storied versions of the 992-generation 911 to create what’s sure to become a storied car (S/C?) itself.

## The S/C Is Fundamentally a GT3 Convertible

Getting Porsche’s highest-revving engine in a convertible 911 once meant scoring an allocation for a Speedster, the last of which [ only 1,948 were built](https://www.motortrend.com/news/2019-porsche-911-speedster-features-options-photos). The 911 GT3 S/C makes those delights more accessible, joining the range without production limits. Saying the GT3 S/C is just a GT3 Cabriolet would be overly simplistic, but it’s a good frame of reference from which to start.

Porsche applies the chassis calibration from [ the GT3 Touring](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2025-porsche-911-gt3-touring-first-test-review) to the S/C, giving it a slightly more pliant—but still plenty raw—character than that of the wing-equipped GT3. __signature detail__Like the GT3, the S/C is powered by a 4.0-liter flat-six engine that revs to 9,000 rpm and produces 502 hp with 311 lb-ft of torque. It’s fired into a raucous idle by an ignition switch to the left of the steering wheel, a detail exclusive to GT3 models as other [ 992.2 911 models](https://www.motortrend.com/news/2025-porsche-911-carrera-coupe-cabriolet-first-look-review) get a starter button. Porsche estimates the GT3 S/C will accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 3.7 seconds and hit a top speed of 194 mph. Carbon-ceramic brakes are standard.

The aero-optimized front and rear bumpers are shared with the GT3, as is the vented hood. As with the GT3 Touring, the extendable rear spoiler features a Gurney flap, also making this the first convertible 911 to have that particular aerodynamic aid.

## A Smattering of S/T Influence

Porsche built just 1,963 copies of [ the epic 911 S/T](https://www.motortrend.com/features/2024-porsche-911-st-first-drive-review), and the GT3 S/C employs parts developed for that rare car. Prominent among those are the carbon-fiber fenders and doors, which feature a distinctive crease trailing from a vent behind the front wheel; the GT3 RS has similar parts. Magnesium wheels measuring 20 inches in front and 21 inches in back are also shared with the 911 S/T.

Inside, the GT3 S/C gets fabric door release pulls, carbon-fiber door handles, lightweight carpets, and a perforated leather-wrapped steering wheel, just like the 911 S/T.

Perhaps the most important piece of the 911 S/T to make its way to the GT3 S/C [ is its transmission](https://www.motortrend.com/features/2024-porsche-911-st-first-look): a six-speed manual with a shortened constant axle ratio and shifter. Unlike other GT3 models, the S/C won’t be offered with a PDK dual-clutch automatic.
