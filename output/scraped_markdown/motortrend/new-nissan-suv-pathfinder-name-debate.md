# The Great New Nissan SUV Name Debate: Will It Be a Pathfinder or Something Else?

A new rugged SUV is coming, but Nissan can’t decide if it should share the Pathfinder name.

[Alisa Priddle](https://www.motortrend.com/staff/alisa-priddle)Writer

![](https://hips.hearstapps.com/hmg-prod/images/d7e622cd-aea4-4e2c-8424-af988563c978.jpg?w=768&width=768&q=75&format=webp)

Nissan has an interesting dilemma on its hands. It has a perfectly good, strong-selling, unibody-constructed [Nissan Pathfinder](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/nissan/pathfinder/2026) that was just updated for the 2026 model year and isn’t retiring anytime soon. But the automaker also has a more rugged, [three-row SUV](https://www.motortrend.com/features/best-3-row-suvs-to-buy) in the works that could be a candidate to wear the Pathfinder badge.

0:00 / 0:00

The forthcoming SUV is one of five vehicles that will be underpinned by Nissan’s all-new body-on-frame platform. The showcase model will be the next [Nissan Xterra](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/nissan/xterra/2029), which is expected by the second half of 2028 with a standard V-6 engine or a hybrid version using the same V-6 under the hood. The new frame architecture will also spawn the next-generation [Nissan Frontier midsize pickup truck](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/nissan/frontier) and more SUVs for the Nissan and Infiniti brands.

Although it would potentially make more than a little sense for the new three-row SUV to use the Pathfinder name in some form, according to Ponz Pandikuthira, Nissan Americas chief planning officer, one thing’s for sure, it will not replace the next Pathfinder. “We already have one that works very well today,” he said.

Launched in 2022, the current Pathfinder is good until at least 2028 before the next-generation model is due. Pandikuthira told *MotorTrend *that it will continue to be a unibody constructed vehicle. “It deserves to be a continued product. It’s not going to be phased out and replaced by something else,” he added.

## Two Similarly Sized SUVs, One Name?

But there could be a scenario where the two similarly sized three-row SUVs will exist in parallel, hence the naming challenge. It’s a subject of intense internal debate.

Chairman and CEO of Nissan Americas Christian Meunier, for one, isn’t opposed to the new body-on-frame SUV wearing a variation on the Pathfinder name. An alpha version of the Pathfinder, if you will, with a name that links the two.

Pandikuthira isn’t a fan of that approach, which he said has the potential to confuse consumers, especially when the vehicles have different missions. There are plenty of negative examples. Ford was criticized for adding Mustang to the Mach-E, although most drop Mustang when referring to the electric crossover. Jeep famously abandoned its plans to break out the Wagoneer name, instead consolidating those models under one Grand Wagoneer flag. Toyota has a Highlander and Grand Highlander. Even Nissan itself went there recently with the Rogue and Rogue Sport—two crossovers that were different sizes and based on different platforms. You get the point.
