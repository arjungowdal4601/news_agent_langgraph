# Nissan Future Product Plans: More Hybrids, Fewer Models, More AI

Nissan’s growth plan calls for streamlining its global lineup.

[Alisa Priddle](https://www.motortrend.com/staff/alisa-priddle)WriterManufacturerPhotographer


It has been a year since Nissan put a new CEO in place and outlined its initial strategy to restore the company to health. Now we are getting a glimpse of the product plan behind the push to return the Japanese automaker to financial vitality in the long term.

0:00 / 0:00

Nissan is streamlining its global portfolio to 45 models from 56 currently, and more than 80 percent of its volume will come from three families of vehicles. There is no list of the low-performing casualties or timeline for the discontinuations yet, but we do know remaining and future models are being classified into four categories or families, defined by their role in the plan.

The global “Core” models will drive volume. By concentrating development around three families, the volume of each model will increase by more than 30 percent. This category includes [the new 2027 Nissan Rogue Hybrid e-Power](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/nissan-e-power-hybrid-prototype-drive-review) that finally comes to North America, having been on sale as the X-Trail hybrid elsewhere in the world for years. It was revealed, in production form, today at an event at Nissan headquarters in Yokohama. It also includes the Juke EV for Europe; it’s pictured at the top of this post.

“Heartbeat” models are designed to embody Nissan’s DNA, imbued with emotion and innovation. [The return of the Nissan Xterra SUV](https://www.motortrend.com/news/2029-nissan-xterra-ev-everything-we-know-future-cars) for the U.S. and Canada (a teaser of which is pictured above) falls into this category. It is on a new body-on-frame platform that will also yield [the next-generation Frontier pickup truck](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/nissan/frontier) and Pathfinder, as well as models for the Infiniti luxury brand. For Japan, one Heartbeat model is the rear-wheel-drive Skyline, which will return as a performance sedan positioned between the Z and GT-R.

Another grouping falls under “Growth” and includes models targeting expansion where demand is emerging.

The final category contains “Partner” models that allow Nissan to extend its market coverage through collaboration, like the Micra with Renault. Although Nissan’s proposed merger with Honda fell through, Nissan remains open to strategic partnerships to get more models into the market.

## New Hybrids Being Developed


Nissan has suffered from not having enough hybrid vehicles, so the plan includes more powertrain options going forward. In addition to e-Power that uses a gas engine as a generator in a series hybrid where 100 percent of the power to the wheels is electric, Nissan is developing a new hybrid system for its forthcoming body-on-frame vehicles (Xterra, Frontier, Pathfinder, and Infiniti QX60).

Plans still call for EVs, but these have been scaled back for North America where demand has cooled. Nissan has canceled a planned entry-level Leaf S for the U.S. for the 2026 model year, and the Ariya was dropped for 2026, as well. Nissan also paused plans to build new EVs for the Nissan and Infiniti brands at its Canton, Mississippi, plant. Engineers continue to work to reduce battery costs, including the development of solid-state batteries.

Infiniti remains key to success. On tap is [the all-new 2027 QX65 SUV](https://www.motortrend.com/news/first-look-2027-infiniti-qx65), a new premium sport sedan, and a luxury hybrid compact SUV based on the Rogue by the end of 2028. There will also be two body-on-frame Infiniti SUVs.
