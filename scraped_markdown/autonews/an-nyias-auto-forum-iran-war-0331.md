Iran war’s high gas prices reveal the auto industry’s class divide

“New-car buyers are intrinsically less vulnerable to fuel prices,” Thomas King, right, president of OEM solutions at JD Power, said at the Automotive Forum. With him, from left, are John Casesa of Guggenheim Partners and Patrick Manzi of the National Automobile Dealers Association. (MOLLY BOIGON/AUTOMOTIVE NEWS)

A panel of executives at the New York International Auto Show urged more stability in Washington, a strong intracontinental trade deal and localized production of compelling vehicles.
