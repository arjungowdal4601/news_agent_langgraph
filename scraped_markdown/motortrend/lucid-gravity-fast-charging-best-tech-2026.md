# Best Tech 2026: The Lucid Gravity Proves That Great Fast-Charging Is About More Than Power

Lucid’s second product can take more power than any other EV, but that’s only one small part of how it’s revolutionizing the public-charging experience.

![](https://hips.hearstapps.com/hmg-prod/images/1c57f4d3-06c3-45da-bc22-9ad6500552c5.jpg?w=768&width=768&q=75&format=webp)

Lucid’s engineers knew they had a problem on their hands from the moment Tesla announced it would open its Supercharger network to competitors in February 2023. By then, the development of the Grand Touring version of its [ Lucid Gravity](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/lucid/gravity) SUV was already some 70 percent complete. But up until that point, the team had treated Tesla’s charging network as an afterthought.

The highest-spec model (for now) of its second EV was based around a 936-volt battery pack that could accept up to a whopping 400 kilowatts of recharging power when plugged into a 1,000-volt fast charger. But at any of Tesla’s nearly 18,000 North American Superchargers, most of which presently top out at 500 volts, the Gravity would be limited to a measly 50 kW. Lucid was on the verge of building a six-figure, luxury SUV with the charging performance of a 10-year-old Nissan Leaf at most Tesla chargers.

![](https://hips.hearstapps.com/hmg-prod/images/01f553b3-ce17-4fab-92cb-1afcd59c21e0.jpg?w=768&width=768&q=75&format=webp)

Emad Dlala, Lucid’s senior vice president of engineering and digital, immediately understood that Lucid couldn’t ignore the nation’s largest and most reliable fast-charging network. “Charging convenience is almost the number one factor for EV adoption,” he told *MotorTrend*. “We can’t underestimate that the Tesla Supercharger network has made Tesla’s success.”

Dlala’s team quickly pivoted and redesigned the Gravity’s charging system in a matter of weeks. They traded the Gravity’s CCS port for [ a Tesla-style NACS port](https://www.motortrend.com/news/best-tech-2025-north-american-charging-system-nacs) and relocated it from the driver’s side front to the driver’s rear fender so owners could use Superchargers without blocking multiple stalls. More crucially, they ditched the DC-to-DC converter that would limit the Grand Touring to 50 kW and routed the power through the rear motor’s windings to boost the 500-volt electricity to the appropriate charging voltage. That rocketed peak charging power to 225 kW when plugged into Tesla’s ubiquitous V3 stations, just below the maximum 250 kW they can deliver.

![](https://hips.hearstapps.com/hmg-prod/images/10437baa-a0a1-4662-8216-94f2ccd6ea17.jpg?w=768&width=768&q=75&format=webp)

Thanks to that eleventh-hour scramble, the Gravity takes a two-pronged approach to road-tripping that earns it a 2026 MotorTrend Best Tech award in the Public Charging Experience category. On the right equipment, the __Gravity Grand Touring is the fastest charging SUV__*MotorTrend *has ever tested. Just 15 minutes on the plug delivers enough juice to cover 194 miles, or nearly three hours of driving, at 70 mph. The incredible 400-kW peak power—higher than any other EV currently on sale—is made possible thanks to the nickel-manganese-cobalt lithium-ion cells that Lucid developed with Panasonic to balance power and energy. Lucid engineers also realized that if it designed the charging software based solely on laboratory testing, it’d leave some performance on the table. Studying how owners of its [ Lucid Air](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/lucid/air) sedan actually use their cars in the real world allowed Lucid to be more aggressive without damaging the battery.
