# Al Oppenheiser, GM’s Mr. Camaro, Calls It a Career

Al Oppenheiser has nothing left to prove after 42 years and some big wins for GM.

[Alisa Priddle](https://www.motortrend.com/staff/alisa-priddle)Writer

![002 Al Oppenheiser Chief Engineer](https://hips.hearstapps.com/mtg-prod/691cd216adc72800021c43c5/002-aloppenheiser-chief-engineer.jpg?w=768&width=768&q=75&format=webp)

[Al Oppenheiser,](https://www.motortrend.com/news/gm-chief-engineer-al-oppenheiser-coty-suvoty-wins-interview) affectionately known as Mr. Camaro, is going to spend more time working on the Camaros in his personal garage. The longtime GM engineer, who has spent 42 years working on everything from muscle cars to electric vehicles, has retired effective today.

0:00 / 0:00

It was a hard decision for the 63-year-old whose daily routine has consisted of getting up at 4 a.m., doing his stretches, and heading to work excited about what the day might bring.

Oppenheiser is a mechanical engineer who spent decades perfecting throaty high-octane muscle cars. He was the chief engineer [for the Chevrolet Camaro](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/chevrolet/camaro), including the ZL1, 1LE, and Z/28, from 2007 through 2018, culminating in [the Camaro being named the 2016 MotorTrend Car of the Year](https://www.motortrend.com/news/chevrolet-camaro-2016-motor-trend-car-of-the-year).

## Killer Camaros and More in Oppenheiser’s Garage

His passion extends to his personal collection that includes a ground-up restoration of [a 1968 Camaro SS Convertible, perfect for the Woodward Dream Cruise](https://www.motortrend.com/features/2018-woodward-dream-cruise-cruising-mr-camaro-al-oppenheiser). He also has a 1968 SS Coupe that the previous owner raced in Australia, a car Oppenheiser hopes to race in the U.S. He also has an original 1970 ½ Camaro RS Z/28, a 1989 Camaro IROC Z/28 Canadian Players Series spare race car, a 2014 Camaro Z/28 (VIN001) and 2018 Camaro ZL1 1LE (VIN001), and a 2024 Camaro ZL1 Convertible Panther Edition. For variety, his garage also stores a 1949 Chevy 5-window 1500 Pickup—a flat black rat rod with air suspension—and a 1931 Model A all steel original with a 4-inch chop and a small block engine.

Engineered the Cadillac Escalade IQ.


Like his car collection, Oppenheiser has had his fingers on other vehicles for General Motors. He began his career with GM in 1985 at the automaker’s Warren, Michigan, GM Technical Center as a test engineer, and parlayed that into engineering positions including the development and validation manager for the Corvette platform.

He gained international experience working on GM’s joint venture vehicles with Toyota, Suzuki, Isuzu, and Daewoo. In 1993 he was promoted to product engineering manager for the Geo Metro and helped develop the Gamma global small program with Opel.

But he wasn’t just a car guy. In 2001 he was chosen to lead the engineering development and launch of [the Chevrolet Colorado](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/chevrolet/colorado)/GMC Canyon—GM’s first midsize trucks. This was followed by a stint engineering and building concept vehicles, including the Camaro Coupe and Convertible. He also ran GM’s Bonneville Salt Flats racing team where GM set nine world speed records with cars powered by an Ecotec engine, and led hot rod builds for celebrities. Before taking the Camaro assignment, he was the director of concept & vehicle integration for the GM Performance Division.
