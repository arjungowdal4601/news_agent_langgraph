# Honda Slashes $7,500 From Price of 2026 Prologue EV SUV

Although Honda has canceled its future EVs, the Prologue remains available for 2026, now starting at $41,000.

[Justin Banner](https://www.motortrend.com/staff/justin-banner)Writer

Although Honda had big plans for its all-electric future, it recently decided to [cancel its plans](https://www.motortrend.com/news/honda-kills-series-0-acura-rsx-evs-for-us) for the 0-series and [the Acura RSX EV SUV](https://www.motortrend.com/news/acura-rsx-prototype-first-look-review). Despite that, there [is an EV SUV](https://www.motortrend.com/rankings/suvs/electric/midsize) still on sale from the Big H: the 2026 [Prologue](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/honda/prologue). The better news is that it will now cost $7,500 cheaper to start than it did as a 2025 model-year vehicle. This means you can hop into an all-electric Honda for less than $42,000 with up to 308 miles of range.

0:00 / 0:00

Although the writing was on the wall for the 0-Series and the Acura RSX, the GM Ultium-partnered Prologue will continue to survive for just a bit longer. It now starts at just $41,395 for the single-motor EX trim that gives you an EV that can cover up to 308 miles on a single charge. The $7,500 reduction is also made across the board, including the dual-motor Prologue variants that deliver up to 294 miles of range.

[The top trim Prologue Elite](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2024-honda-prologue-electric-suv-first-test-review) will set you back the most at $51,895, and reduces your range to 283 miles due to its standard 21-inch wheels (lower trims get 19-inch wheels as standard equipment). But you will be able to use [a DC fast charger](https://www.motortrend.com/features/fastest-charging-evs) at up to a 150-kW output, charging from 20–80 percent in 35 minutes. You’ll also be able to use a NACS charger (like a Tesla Supercharger) with the available Honda-approved NACS-to-CCS DC adapter. Of course, you can skip that by plugging the Prologue into a Level 2 240-volt AC charger or by relying on Level 1 110-volt AC charging to have a fully charged vehicle when you wake up in the morning.

![2025 Honda Prologue in motion](https://hips.hearstapps.com/mtg-prod/67c5d837a5bd9f0008577cc1/2025hondaprologueinmotion.jpg?crop=1xw:1xh;center,top&resize=1920:*&w=768&width=768&q=100&format=webp)

The price cut is a welcome development, but we don’t know how long Honda will continue to build [the Prologue](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2024-honda-prologue-ev-first-drive-review). This collaboration between the Japanese automaker and GM was only supposed to last until Honda began its own in-house EV production. Now that those plans are canceled for the U.S., the company has said its focus will be on hybrids like [the Prelude](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2026-honda-prelude-first-drive-review), CR-V Hybrid, and [Civic Hybrid](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2025-honda-civic-sedan-sport-touring-hybrid-yearlong-review-update-6-winter-test). But at least you’ll be able to get a good deal on Honda’s sole all-electric SUV while it lasts.
