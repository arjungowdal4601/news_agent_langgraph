# Hyundai’s Boulder Concept Signals a Rugged, Body-on-Frame SUV and Truck Future

Hyundai’s big reveal in New York serves as a bellwether of its coming truck-based future.

[Mike Floyd](https://www.motortrend.com/staff/mike-floyd)WriterManufacturerPhotographer

[Hyundai](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/hyundai) used the Big Apple to make one of its biggest new product splashes in recent years. At the [2026 New York auto show](https://www.motortrend.com/new-york-auto-show), the Korean automaker, which is celebrating 40 years in America, took the wraps off of its body-on-frame-constructed Hyundai Boulder Concept.

0:00 / 0:00

The reveal event featured a splash of intrigue and drama beforehand. Prior to the show in advance of what it billed as a “major world debut,” Hyundai teased a square block of molten-looking rock floating over a barren landscape with a lone person barely visible in the frame, assuring everyone it was “no joke,” after all, given everything was going down on April Fool’s Day. See, a Boulder. Get it?

It’s far from the first time Hyundai has used an American town to name a vehicle; think [Santa Fe](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2022-hyundai-santa-cruz-yearlong-review-verdict) and Santa Cruz. The Boulder Concept was styled at the company’s U.S. design center in California; the production vehicle will be built in America with American steel. Speaking of, the Hyundai design team’s philosophy is what it dubbed “Art of Steel,” and to be sure, there’s a lot of creased, bold steel to the concept’s design, and it’s sprayed in a finish called Liquid Titanium.

The Boulder rolls on super-aggressive 37-inch tires and boasts advanced digital cameras and software to get you where you want to go on the trail, as well as what it calls Safari fixed upper windows that are designed to let in more light, a power-down rear window, and a side-hinge tailgate door that opens from either side. An accessory-ready platform will allow Boulder owners to customize their vehicles. [For more on the Boulder’s features, click here.](https://www.motortrend.com/news/hyundai-boulder-concept-top-features)

Everywhere you look on the Boulder Concept, you see off-road-ready gear, including a light bank at the top of the vehicle, a low-profile roof rack, a full-size spare tire wrapped in one of its 37- x 12-inch 50R18 LT, all-terrain tires, tow hooks, and massive, bulky fenders to house those giant wheels. It all lends it a super-aggressive appearance. At first glance, the Hyundai Boulder looks like a vehicle that will be designed to give the Broncos, Wranglers, and 4Runners of the world everything they can handle. And it stands to reason that the Boulder will help expand Hyundai’s emerging XRT off-road-themed subbrand.

Step inside via a coach door opening, and the interior renderings also look the part of an aggressive, off-road-ready machine, with something of a rolled-look design, four separate screens, lots of buttons, and a flat-bottom steering wheel. There’s a bit of a futuristic edge to it as well.

As for what will power the eventual production version of the Hyundai Boulder Concept, it’s anyone’s guess at this point, though Hyundai announced at the New York show that it will roll out an EREV powertrain, in which a battery pack and electric motors power the vehicle, but a gas engine provides extra motivation and range when the battery becomes depleted. This vastly extends the vehicle’s overall range, and it stands to reason it would make sense in this application.

The automaker’s body-on-frame platform will also help usher in Hyundai’s larger truck, which is expected by 2030. Hyundai showed a truck under a sheet during its press conference, so it’s most definitely coming, and it will also no doubt be designed to be as rugged as the Boulder.

Hyundai is already the fifth-largest brand in America, but it is looking for more; it claims 36 new or refreshed vehicles are coming by 2030, with 80 percent of those models built here in the U.S. You can no doubt count the production version of the Boulder as one of those models.
