## 2027 Genesis GV60 Expert Review

Reviewed by [Alex Leanse](https://www.motortrend.com/staff/alex-leanse)

Although the GV60 is Genesis’ only dedicated electric vehicle, it approaches luxury like the Korean automaker’s other models. The GV60 debuted for model year 2022, putting an upscale spin on fundamental hardware shared with the Hyundai Ioniq 5 and Kia EV6.

Genesis gave the GV60 a refresh for 2026, and for 2027 a new high-performance Magma variant is arriving. Other [ electric luxury SUVs](https://www.motortrend.com/rankings/suvs/electric) include the Cadillac Optiq, Audi Q6 E-Tron, and BMW iX3.

## What’s New

For 2026, Genesis updated the GV60 with highlights including revised exterior design, a bigger battery with Tesla-style NACS charging, and a large 27-inch digital display. For 2027, the main change for the GV60 is the addition of the high-performance Magma model, which promises powerful acceleration and sporty handling like no Genesis has had before.

The 2027 GV60 Magma offers as much as 641 hp and 583 lb-ft, good for a 0-60 mph sprint of about 3.0 seconds. If the GV60 Magma is anywhere near as good as the Ioniq 5 N on the same platform, Genesis will have a winner on its hands.

The exterior design is [ quirky yet distinctive](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2026-genesis-gv60-performance-first-test-review), showing fresh thinking against rivals more bound by tradition. Likewise, the cabin has cool flourishes and details that would be too daring for some brands. Tech is impressive and build quality is strong, but some materials

For drivers who can work with those constraints, the GV60 is a nice option.

## MotorTrend Tested

The GV60’s base powertrain uses a single RWD electric motor that produces 225 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque. With this, 0-60 mph acceleration should take about 6.5 seconds.

Three levels of dual-motor AWD powertrains are offered. The starter level makes 314 hp and 446 lb-ft of torque, good for a 0-60 mph run of about 4.4 seconds.

Next up is the Performance grade, which increases output to 429 hp and 516 lb-ft of torque, enabling a 3.7-second 0-60 mph time in *MotorTrend *testing.

This year, Genesis introduces the GV60 Magma, which can produce as much as 641 hp and 583 lb-ft. That should allow it to launch to 60 mph in around 3.0 seconds.

## EV Range and Charging

For 2026, Genesis made an 84.0 kWh battery standard, an upgrade from the 77.4 kWh battery used previously. An [ integrated NACS port](https://www.motortrend.com/news/best-tech-2025-north-american-charging-system-nacs) was also added, allowing the GV60 to plug in at Tesla Supercharger stations.

The maximum charge rate of about 240 kW is impressive, allowing for fast charging. In *MotorTrend *testing, the GV60 gained 142 miles in the first 15 minutes after plugging in, and 191 miles after 30 minutes.

Driving range is estimated at 306 miles for the RWD model, between 267 and 282 miles for the AWD model, and 252 miles for the Performance AWD model. However, in MotorTrend’s Road Trip Range Test, the GV60 Performance AWD went only 214 miles on the highway before needing to plug in.

### Available Features

Higher-end GV60 models add blind-spot monitoring cameras, and upgraded adaptive cruise control with semi-autonomous lane change capability.

## Cargo Space and Interior Room

Passenger space in the GV60 suffices, but its cargo capacity is a bit less than some of its segment competitors. There’s a small 0.71 cubic foot frunk in addition to the cabin space.
