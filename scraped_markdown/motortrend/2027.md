## 2027 Ford Expedition Expert Review

Reviewed by [Bob Hernandez](https://www.motortrend.com/staff/bob-hernandez)

The Ford Expedition has spent the last few decades evolving from a rugged, F-150-based workhorse into a sophisticated multi-tool for the modern American family. For 2027, Ford doubles down on this mission, balancing massive towing capacity and truck-based durability with a high-tech cabin that finally feels as premium as its price tag suggests.

In a segment where size is a prerequisite but refinement is the new battlefield, the latest Expedition aims to prove it can out-luxury the Chevy Tahoe, Nissan Armada, and Toyota Sequoia without losing its blue-collar backbone.

## What We Think

The Expedition masterfully bridges its rugged heritage with a futuristic digital experience, solidifying its place as a premier choice for large families.

Though the 2027 model sticks with a twin-turbo V-6, the powertrain delivers effortless, punchy torque that masks the vehicle's massive footprint. On the open road, [ the ride is exceptionally plush](https://www.motortrend.com/news/ford-expedition-2026-suvoty), though the driving dynamics remain purely utilitarian.

The steering feels somewhat disconnected and slow, while the brake pedal lacks the linear transparency found in some rivals. Nevertheless, it excels as a long-distance cruiser, especially with the latest version of BlueCruise providing a seamless hands-free experience.

The interior has undergone a radical transformation, dominated by a massive 24-inch panoramic display and a [ versatile "Split Gate" tailgate](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2025-ford-expedition-first-drive-review) that redefines cargo-area utility. While the cabin is resplendent with high-quality materials and thoughtful touches like the "Flex Powered Console," some ergonomic choices, such as the digital-heavy interface for certain climate and vehicle controls, require a learning curve.

Ultimately, this generation of the Expedition proves that a traditional body-on-frame SUV can embrace modern tech without sacrificing the massive towing capacity and interior volume that defined its predecessors.

## Top-Ranked Competitors

## MotorTrend Tested

The Ford Expedition’s powertrain lineup carries over for 2027, anchored by a standard 3.5-liter twin-turbo V-6 producing 400 hp and 480 lb-ft of torque. A high-output variant is also available, boosting output to 440 hp and 510 lb-ft.

In MotorTrend testing, an Expedition Tremor equipped with the high-output engine hit 60 mph in just 5.3 seconds, an impressive feat for such a heavy SUV.

For comparison, the Toyota Sequoia relies exclusively on a hybrid system that pairs a 3.4-liter twin-turbo V-6 with an electric motor for a combined 437 hp and a stout 583 lb-ft of torque. Despite the Toyota's torque advantage, its MotorTrend-tested 0–60 mph times for 4WD models ranged from 5.6 to 6.2 seconds, trailing the top-spec Ford.

## MPGs and Range

The 2027 Ford Expedition should continue to prioritize power over efficiency, delivering an expected EPA-estimated fuel economy of 16/24 mpg city/highway for RWD models and 15/22 mpg for 4WD variants. With a total driving range of 441 miles (RWD) and 418 miles (4WD), the Expedition remains a traditional, gas-only heavyweight in a segment increasingly focused on electrification.

In contrast, the Toyota Sequoia’s standard hybrid powertrain offers a clear advantage in urban efficiency. While both SUVs achieve an identical 24 mpg on the highway in RWD configuration, the Sequoia’s 21 mpg city rating and 495-mile maximum range easily outpace the Ford. For 4WD buyers, the Toyota maintains its lead with 19/22 mpg.

The Chevrolet Tahoe is a more direct match for the 2027 Expedition in terms of efficiency. With a 5.3-liter V-8, the 4WD Tahoe comes in at 15/19 mpg and 408 miles of range, below the Expedition. Spring for the 6.2-liter V-8 and the Tahoe’s figures are 14/18 mpg and 384 miles of range.

## Safety Features

The 2027 Ford Expedition should come equipped with a comprehensive suite of standard driver-assist features designed to enhance safety, including:

- Adaptive cruise control
- Automatic emergency braking
- Lane keeping
- Blind-spot monitoring (with cross-traffic alert and trailer coverage)
- Intersection assist (detects oncoming traffic during left turns)
- 360 camera
- Automatic high beams

## Cargo Space and Interior Room

The 2027 Expedition occupies a middle ground in the full-size segment, offering superior legroom and significantly more maximum cargo volume than the Toyota Sequoia. While it can't quite match the Chevrolet Tahoe’s class-leading total space, the Expedition’s generous third-row legroom makes it a more comfortable long-haul option for adult passengers.

When you need maximum space, Ford offers the 2027 Expedition Max to compete with the Chevrolet Suburban and GMC Yukon XL.

## Payload and Towing Capacity

When properly equipped with the Heavy-Duty Trailer Tow package and 4WD, this Expedition boasts a maximum towing capacity of up to 9,600 lbs. Payload capacity ranges between 1,640 and 1,900 pounds depending on the configuration, with higher capacities on base models.

## Technology

### Infotainment

The 2027 Ford Expedition features a striking 24.0-inch panoramic display positioned near the windshield to handle primary driving data, maps, and media at a glance.

This is paired with a centrally located 13.2-inch touchscreen for climate and infotainment adjustments, while [ Apple CarPlay](https://www.motortrend.com/features/apple-carplay-tips-and-secrets),

[, and four USB ports come standard across the lineup.](https://www.motortrend.com/features/android-auto-tips-and-tricks)

__Android Auto__### Sound System

The base model comes standard with a six-speaker sound system. Other available, upgraded sound systems on higher trims include 10- and 22-speaker setups from Bang & Olufsen.

### Available Upgrades

The Expedition offers several premium upgrades, including 22-inch wheels and a hands-free power liftgate for added exterior style and convenience. The cabin can be tailored for maximum utility with eight-passenger seating or enhanced comfort via second-row captain’s chairs and heated and ventilated leather-trimmed seats.

The split rear tailgate may be helpful in making sure groceries don’t fall out when you open the trunk, but shorter drivers may have difficulty accessing cargo and, when they do, may dirty their clothes as they reach inside.

For convenience on long drives, BlueCruise provides a hands-free driving experience on compatible highways.

## Value

### Standard Features

The Expedition delivers strong value by pairing its robust twin-turbo powertrain with a ride quality that remains impressively plush over long distances. Inside, the cabin justifies its price point with an expansive digital layout and premium materials, offering the high-end feel and immense interior volume that modern families prioritize.

If, however, you don’t need the towing capacity or immense size of the 2027 Expedition, also consider a loaded Explorer for far less money.
