What We Think

The GV70 is Genesis' most popular vehicle, and [ deservedly so](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2022-genesis-gv70-yearlong-test-review-verdict). The GV70 stands out in the compact luxury crossover class with unique design, a rich cabin, and engaging road manners.

Small changes made for 2026—fresh styling, updated tech, and quality of life improvements—[ improved](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2026-genesis-gv70-first-drive-review) an already great vehicle. This attention to detail shows that Genesis is attuned to the wants and needs of luxury SUV shoppers.

From behind the wheel, the GV70’s turbocharged engines deliver solid performance, but not great fuel efficiency. There’s no hybrid version, although Genesis has offered [a full EV](https://www.motortrend.com/news/2023-genesis-gv70-electrified-suvoty-review). Chassis tuning leans more toward comfort than engagement, though there's enough athleticism to keep things interesting. Hydraulic chassis bushings added for 2026 enhanced refinement.

Driver-assist tech also performs well. Capacitive sensing in the steering wheel detects the driver’s hands, allowing for a more relaxed highway experience.

There are a few drawbacks. Revised steering calibration has added weight without much more feel. While the brakes are effective, the pedal feels a bit [ numb and non-linear](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2026-genesis-gv70-first-test-review).

Genesis’ gradual improvements keep the GV70 easy to recommend. It's quiet, comfortable, and packed with features, strengthening the SUV's case against [ established luxury rivals](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2023-acura-rdx-vs-genesis-gv70-vs-lexus-nx-350-vs-lincoln-corsair-compact-luxury-suv-comparison-test-review).

## MotorTrend Tested

Every GV70 uses an eight-speed automatic transmission and AWD. The base engine is a 2.5-liter turbocharged I-4 that produces 300 hp and 311 lb-ft of torque. In *MotorTrend *testing, a GV70 with this engine accelerated from 0-60 mph in 6.0 seconds.

The GV70 is available with a 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V-6 developing 375 hp and 391 lb-ft of torque. With this engine, the GV70 hit 60 mph in 5.2 seconds in *MotorTrend *testing.

## MPGs and Range

Fuel economy for the GV70 is only fine; the lack of a hybrid option is notable. Expect the I-4 engine to get about 20/26-28 mpg city/highway. With the V-6, efficiency falls to 18/25 mpg.

Driving range is as high as 400 miles with the I-4 engine, and as low as 365 miles with the V-6. Some competitors offer both greater efficiency and range.

## The All-Electric GV70

Genesis offers an all-electric version of the GV70, and it might be the best option in the lineup. The GV70 Electric has a strong dual-motor powertrain that enables a 0-60 mph time of just 3.7 seconds. Ride and handling are as smooth as the gas-powered model, but the EV is even quieter.

Driving range isn’t excellent, coming in at slightly more than 200 miles, but a built-in NACS port allows for easy charging. [ Read all the details on the GV70 Electric here](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/genesis/gv70-electric).

## Value

### Standard Features

The base 2027 GV70 starts at about $51,000 and is powered by the I-4 engine. Outside, it includes 19-inch wheels, LED lighting, and a power-operated tailgate. Inside, it has leatherette upholstery, heated front seats, a 27-inch digital display, and multicolor ambient lighting.

### Recommended Trim

Although the I-4 engine gets the job done, the GV70 drives much better with the optional V-6. The Sport Advanced trim with this engine costs about $66,000 but also gains niceties like a panoramic sunroof, front and rear parking sensors, leather upholstery, heated and ventilated front seats, a heated steering wheel, and 360-degree camera.
