Yunex Traffic has been appointed as the UK warranty and service partner for electric vehicle (EV) charge point operator Believ, providing nationwide maintenance and support across a network of more than 2,300 sockets deployed at businesses, public spaces and on-street residential locations. The partnership is intended to support Believ’s aim of meeting the UK Government’s 99% reliability target under the new Public Charge Point regulations.

Believ’s network features AC and DC rapid chargers from manufacturers including Wallbox and Etrel, and operates entirely on renewable energy. Further installations are already scheduled in Brighton, Suffolk and Leeds as part of an ongoing rollout. Yunex Traffic will deliver planned and reactive maintenance through a team of NICEIC-accredited engineers operating from 15 regional service centres, backed by a 24/7 service operations centre providing remote monitoring and incident management.

In a statement, Guy Bartlett, Chief Executive of Believ, said: “We are thrilled to be working with Yunex Traffic, ensuring that our maintenance capacity grows with our network. Reliability is essential to ensuring drivers realise the convenience and benefits of driving an EV and we will therefore continue to invest in the best technology and services to support optimum reliability.”

Wilke Reints, Managing Director of Yunex Traffic UK, added: “We’re proud to be appointed Believ’s UK service partner, and to be supporting a company that shares our commitment to sustainability and accessibility. As our EVCI capabilities continue to expand, our experienced teams are equipped to deliver comprehensive support, from site surveys and civil works to installation, proactive and reactive maintenance, and parts supply.”
