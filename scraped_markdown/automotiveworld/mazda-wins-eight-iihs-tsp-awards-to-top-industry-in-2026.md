Mazda has earned eight 2026 Top Safety Pick+ (TSP+) awards from the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety (IIHS), more than any other brand and the third consecutive year the automaker has led the industry in the highest tier of IIHS recognition. The winning models are the Mazda3 Sedan, Mazda3 Hatchback, CX-30, CX-50, CX-70, CX-70 PHEV, CX-90 and CX-90 PHEV.

The 2026 IIHS criteria introduced tougher requirements for pedestrian crash prevention, back-seat protection and crash-avoidance performance. Mazda equips all new vehicles with blind spot monitoring, rear cross traffic alert, automatic emergency braking and lane departure warning as standard. The Mazda3 starts at US$24,550 and the 2026 CX-5 at US$29,990, with the company noting that advanced safety features are available without a premium price.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/25123937/mazda-iihs-tspp-2026-1024x640.jpg)


Mazda has now earned 99 IIHS Top Safety awards since 2008, of which 73 have been TSP+.

In a statement, David Harkey, President of IIHS, said: “Mazda continues to excel in safety with eight IIHS TOP SAFETY PICK+ models this year. They are providing consumers with the best in safety features at a range of prices.”

Jennifer Morrison, Director of Vehicle Safety Strategy at Mazda North American Operations, added: “Earning eight TOP SAFETY PICK+ awards shows what happens when you stay true to your philosophy: driving stays engaging, and families stay protected.”
