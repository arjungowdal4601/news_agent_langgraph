Nobina has been awarded a new city bus contract by HSL, the Helsinki regional transport authority, covering 69 scheduled buses across four separate agreements in the Helsinki metropolitan area. The majority of the fleet will comprise fully electric vehicles, with traffic on three of the contracts commencing in August 2027.

The three larger contracts carry a seven-year term with an option for a three-year extension. A fourth contract, covering six vehicles, runs for two years with an option for a further two-year extension. The agreements extend Nobina’s existing presence in the Helsinki region as HSL continues its push toward lower-emission public transport.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/25091259/nobina-helsinki-city-service-1024x660.jpg)


In a statement, Topi Simola, Managing Director of Nobina Finland, said: “We are very proud of the trust placed in us by HSL. Together, we continue to develop a sustainable, reliable, and modern public transport system for the Helsinki metropolitan area. Our joint efforts aim to strengthen the region’s attractiveness and the role of public transport in the green transition.”

The award decision may be appealed until 7 April 2026.
