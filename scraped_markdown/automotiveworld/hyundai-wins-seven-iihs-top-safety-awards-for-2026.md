Hyundai has secured six 2026 Top Safety Pick+ (TSP+) and one Top Safety Pick (TSP) award from the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety (IIHS), under criteria that this year introduced tougher requirements for back seat passenger protection and crash avoidance systems. The TSP+ recipients are the Ioniq 5, Ioniq 9, Sonata, Kona, Tucson and Santa Fe, while the Elantra received the TSP designation.

To qualify for either award in 2026, vehicles must achieve a “good” rating in the moderate front overlap test, a stricter threshold than the “acceptable” rating that sufficed for the TSP in 2025. TSP+ additionally requires good ratings in the small overlap front, side and pedestrian front crash prevention tests, along with an acceptable or good rating in the updated vehicle-to-vehicle 2.0 front crash prevention evaluation.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/25113738/iihs-tso-status.jpg)


In a statement, David Harkey, President of IIHS, said: “This year, we’re asking automakers to make excellent protection for back seat passengers the norm. For Top Safety Pick+, we’re requiring crash avoidance systems that are better at preventing pedestrian crashes as well as higher speed crashes with other vehicles.”

Separately, eleven 2026 Hyundai models have received five-star overall safety ratings under the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration’s (NHTSA) New Car Assessment Program, including the Elantra, Sonata, Ioniq 6, Ioniq 9, Tucson, Santa Cruz and Santa Fe across standard and hybrid variants.
