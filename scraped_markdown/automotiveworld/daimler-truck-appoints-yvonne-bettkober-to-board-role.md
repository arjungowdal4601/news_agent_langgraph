The Supervisory Board of Daimler Truck Holding AG has appointed Yvonne Bettkober as a Member of the Board of Management, effective 1 October 2026, to lead human resources across the Daimler Truck Group and serve as labour director, succeeding Jürgen Hartwig who will leave the company at the end of November 2026 after more than 30 years. Bettkober joins from Audi AG, where she served as Chief Transformation Officer, with her initial term running until 30 September 2029.

Her appointment follows the planned departure of Jürgen Hartwig, who will leave at his own request at the end of November 2026 after more than 30 years with the company. Hartwig has held the human resources board portfolio since 2016 and has served as a Member of the Board of Management since December 2021.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/25090608/daimler-truck-yvonne-bettkober-276x300.jpg)

Before Audi, Bettkober held senior roles at Microsoft, Amazon Web Services and CARIAD, Volkswagen Group’s software unit, where she led transformation, organisational development and advisory functions at board level.

In a statement, Karin Rådström, President, Chief Executive and Chair of the Board of Management of Daimler Truck Holding AG, said: “Jürgen has been a defining force in shaping our culture at Daimler Truck. His leadership was critical during our spinoff and launch as an independent company. I am excited to welcome Yvonne Bettkober to our Board of Management. With our strategy set and our simpler, faster, stronger culture taking hold, her international experience, strategic clarity, and execution strength will be key as we move forward.”
