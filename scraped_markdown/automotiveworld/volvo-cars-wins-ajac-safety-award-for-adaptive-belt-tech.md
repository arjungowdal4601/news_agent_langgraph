The AJAC Innovation Awards recognise advancements in automotive technology judged by a panel of automotive journalists. In a statement, Graham Heeps, Chair of the AJAC Innovation Awards, said: “Volvo has an unmatched track record in advancing protection for humans both inside and outside the car: its multi-adaptive safety belt is the latest in a string of safety innovations that goes back decades. The AJAC Innovation Awards jurors applaud this latest in a string of moves to democratise safety, aiming to provide equal protection to all vehicle occupants, no matter their size, shape or age.”

Matt Girgis, Managing Director of Volvo Car Canada, added: “This new technology is a major evolution to the three-point safety belt, building on a legacy that began in 1959 and redefining how we use real-time data to better protect every occupant, in every real-world scenario.”

The system works alongside airbags, occupant detection and driver assistance systems. Online configurations and deposits for the EX60 are available in Canada.
