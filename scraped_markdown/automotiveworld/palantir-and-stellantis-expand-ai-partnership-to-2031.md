Stellantis has renewed and expanded its data and artificial intelligence partnership with Palantir Technologies for a further five years, extending a collaboration that began in 2016. Under the new agreement, Stellantis will broaden its use of Palantir Foundry and begin deploying the Palantir Artificial Intelligence Platform (AIP) across select business functions and regions.

Foundry provides a unified environment for managing and operationalising data within complex industrial operations, while AIP offers tools to integrate artificial intelligence (AI) capabilities into existing workflows in a governed manner. The combined deployment supports Stellantis teams in consolidating fragmented datasets, improving transparency and enabling faster decision-making.

The integration of AIP builds on Stellantis’ established data ontology within Foundry, connecting generative AI capabilities to the automaker’s internal data, business rules and operational processes. Palantir says the approach strengthens governance and traceability, and supports the controlled scaling of AI use cases across the organisation. It also contributes to Stellantis’ Data4All programme, which aims to broaden safe data access across the company.

“We are proud to deepen our partnership with Stellantis and to support them as they define what the AI-powered industrial enterprise of tomorrow looks like,” said François Bohuon, General Manager of Palantir France and EMEA Executive, and Grégoire Omont, Europe Operations Lead. “By combining Foundry and AIP, we are helping Stellantis embed secure, governed AI at the heart of its operations, turning data into a decisive advantage across every function and geography.”
