Renault has launched RMC BFM Drive, an AI-generated in-car radio service developed with RMC BFM Group, available from March 2026 on vehicles equipped with the automaker’s OpenR Link with Google built-in multimedia system. The app creates personalised audio sessions based on driver-selected preferences including topic, journey length, preferred presenter and regional news.

Users choose from editorial themes spanning politics, economics, sport, culture and technology, with content automatically updated throughout the journey. The service is free of charge and downloadable via the Play Store on compatible OpenR Link vehicles.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/25084303/renault-rmc-bfm-drive-1024x683.jpg)


In a statement, Arnaud Belloni, Global Chief Marketing Officer at Renault, said: “Through our partnership and the RMC BFM Drive app, we are bringing customers a unique audio experience with personalised, contextualised content delivered straight to their speakers, tailored to each journey. We are harnessing AI to give users access to an exclusive, localised selection of the best editorial content of RMC BFM Group, based on their interests and listening time. This unique partnership combines the very best in content and innovation to enhance the in-car experience.”

Claire Leost, Chief Executive of CMA Media, added: “We believe that the combination of technology, mobility and audio content offered by RMC BFM Drive will transform our relationship with our audience over the long term. Building closer ties with our listeners is key to our mission, and this is further underlined by this AI-based innovation.”
