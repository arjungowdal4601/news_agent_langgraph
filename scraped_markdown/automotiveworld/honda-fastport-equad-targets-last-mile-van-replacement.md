Honda's B2B micromobility venture Fastport is showcasing its all-electric eQuad at the New York International Auto Show in April

Honda’s B2B micromobility venture Fastport will showcase its all-electric eQuad cargo vehicle and Fleet-as-a-Service platform at the New York International Auto Show during Media Days on 1 and 2 April. The eQuad is designed to replace conventional delivery vans in urban last-mile logistics and is engineered for bike-lane use, combining a pedal-by-wire assist system with swappable Honda Mobile Power Pack batteries.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/31192912/honda-electric-fastport-equad-new-york-international-auto-show-1024x683.jpg)


The zero-emission vehicle features regenerative braking, modular cargo configurations and an enclosed rider cabin, and incorporates software-defined vehicle (SDV) technology. Fastport positions the eQuad as a direct replacement for traditional delivery vans operating in dense urban environments.

Fastport Chief of Operations Jamie Davies will lead a press walk-around and demonstration at the show’s EV Hybrid Test Track on 1 April, with demo rides available across both media days.
