Mercedes-Benz Trucks and FAUN are showcasing a circular economy refuse truck at IFAT, built partly from recycled materials with 31 partners

Mercedes-Benz Trucks and FAUN Umwelttechnik are presenting a circular economy concept vehicle at IFAT in Munich, developed alongside 31 partners from industry and the recycling sector. Based on the battery-electric Mercedes-Benz eEconic, the refuse collection truck is partly constructed from recycled materials while being purpose-built to collect materials for recycling in operation.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/25084851/mercedes-benz-eeconic-sustainability-project-1024x683.jpg)


The concept demonstrates how materials and industrial processes can be designed to conserve resources and close material cycles, with procurement and production more closely integrated with comprehensive recycling at each stage. As a refuse collection vehicle, it embodies the circular approach directly: the truck handles recyclable materials in service while its own construction draws on them.

The 31-partner consortium spans industry and the recycling sector, and the group intends to apply insights from the project toward transferring more sustainable material and production concepts into series production. Further details are expected in the run-up to IFAT.
