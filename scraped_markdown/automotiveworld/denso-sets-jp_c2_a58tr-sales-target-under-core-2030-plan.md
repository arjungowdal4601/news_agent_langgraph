Denso has adopted a new five-year mid-term management plan, CORE 2030, covering fiscal years 2027 to 2031, targeting sales revenue of more than JP¥8tr (US$50.3bn) and an operating margin above 10% by fiscal year 2031. The plan centres on three pillars: product development for a diversifying mobility market, AI-integrated manufacturing, and co-creation with partners in agriculture and factory automation.

Denso will invest JP¥3.7tr (US$23.3bn) in research and development over the plan period, targeting electrification and intelligence sales of approximately JP¥4tr (US$25.2bn), around 1.5 times current levels. Specific targets include JP¥1.9tr (US$11.9bn) in electrification sales and JP¥1.0tr (US$6.3bn) in ADAS-related revenue by fiscal year 2031.

On manufacturing, Denso is building the Zenmyo-Minami Plant in Nishio City, Aichi Prefecture, due for completion in 2027, where AI-integrated production processes will be implemented. Beyond automotive, the company targets JP¥100bn (US$629m) in agricultural sector sales and JP¥300bn (US$1.9bn) in factory automation by 2030, applying automotive-grade reliability technology to address industrial workforce and productivity challenges.

The plan sets a return on equity target above 11% and aims for Scope 1 and 2 carbon neutrality with credits during the plan period, with a target for carbon neutrality without credits by fiscal year 2036.
