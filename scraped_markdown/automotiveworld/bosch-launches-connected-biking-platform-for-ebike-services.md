Bosch eBike Systems has launched the Connected Biking platform, a digital ecosystem enabling eBike manufacturers, dealers, fleet operators, leasing providers and insurance companies to develop and operate their own services around connected electric bikes. The platform connects via standardised interfaces and cloud APIs, with data collected continuously through the Bosch ConnectModule covering status, configuration, usage and service information across the full eBike lifecycle.

Fleet operators gain real-time location, state-of-charge and usage data for each unit, with remote motor disabling available in the event of theft. Leasing providers can track condition and service history across a contract term to support returns appraisal and remarketing, while insurance companies can use alarm and GPS tracking functions to accelerate claims processing. Mobility platform partners including allride, Connected Cycle, Friiway, Joyride and OKGO are already using the platform for fleet and hire management, and navigation app komoot is also integrated.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/25120119/bosch-ebike_connected-biking-platform-1024x683.jpg)


All data processed by Bosch eBike Systems is stored on European servers under European data protection law, with over-the-air updates used to maintain security.

In a statement, Claus Fleischer, Chief Executive of Bosch eBike Systems, said: “Our Connected Biking platform is becoming a central component of the digital ecosystem around the eBike. We are creating opportunities for industry players to develop their own digital offerings. The first partners are already connected and are shaping connected eBike mobility together with us.”
