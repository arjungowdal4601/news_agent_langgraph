Workhorse Group has launched a 140 kWh battery variant of its W56 electric step van, offering an estimated range of 100 miles per charge at full payload and a starting price of US$169,000. The new configuration is positioned as a lower-cost entry point for last-mile delivery fleets operating shorter urban routes, sitting alongside the existing 210 kWh version of the W56.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/26172419/workhorse-w56-electric-step-van-1024x682.jpg)


The standard 178-inch wheelbase model provides 1,000 cubic feet of cargo space and a payload of 11,000 lbs. An extended 208-inch wheelbase variant offers the same cargo volume with a 10,000 lb payload. Both configurations use the same platform as the existing W56, with a fully integrated composite body produced on site at Workhorse’s Union City, Indiana facility, which has a stated capacity of more than 5,000 vehicles per year on a single shift.

The model follows Workhorse’s December 2025 merger with Motiv Electric Trucks, with the company citing economies of scale from the combined operation as a factor in the lower price point. In a statement, Scott Griffith, Chief Executive of Workhorse, said: “Commercial ground fleets have similar exposure to spikes in fuel prices as airlines, and the launch of this new model offers fleets a no-compromise option to control costs while still ensuring efficient operations. Because electricity costs are low, local, and more immune to global oil shocks, we believe every electric truck in a mixed fleet can act as a buffer against the volatility that is once again hammering operators who run entirely on gasoline and diesel.”
