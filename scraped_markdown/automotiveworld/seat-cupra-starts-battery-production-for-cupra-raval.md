SEAT & Cupra has begun series production of battery systems at its Martorell plant in Spain, marking the final preparatory step before electric vehicle manufacturing starts at the facility this summer. The batteries, built using Volkswagen Group’s Unified Cell platform, will power both the Cupra Raval and the Volkswagen ID. Polo, the two electric models set to be manufactured at the site.

The 64,000 m² assembly facility can produce 1,200 battery systems per day and up to 300,000 annually, with completed units transported automatically via a 600-metre bridge directly to the vehicle production line. The plant employs 500 people, uses cell-to-pack technology across two parallel lines generating 3,600 stacks per day, and draws 70% of its electricity from 11,000 roof-mounted solar panels. The E Box battery management unit is produced at SEAT & Cupra’s El Prat Components facility, which previously manufactured manual gearboxes for 45 years.

“The start of production of series assembly battery systems marks a decisive milestone in the final stretch of the transformation of our company and takes us closer to the series production of the Cupra Raval,” said Markus Haupt, Chief Executive of SEAT & Cupra. “With the beginning of electric-vehicle production, Martorell will strengthen its position as a fully flexible plant, ready to lead Spain’s transition to electric mobility.”

André Kleb, Chief Production Officer for the Iberian Peninsula at Volkswagen Group’s Brand Group Core, added: “Through the Electric Urban Car Family project, we will manufacture four electric models in Spain, starting with the Cupra Raval, making electric mobility accessible from the Iberian Peninsula to customers across Europe.”
