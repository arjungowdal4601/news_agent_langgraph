GAC International Philippines has consolidated its GAC Motor, Aion and Hyptec product lines into a unified commercial framework under a strategy called ONE GAC 2.0, as the company advances a shift to direct self-operated business in the country. The reorganisation focuses on a single brand identity, integrated sales channel and unified service standard across its Philippine dealer network.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/26124154/gac-philippines-1024x682.jpg)


The strategy was announced in late January 2026, followed by a media and influencer content event at its Makati showroom in early February, where the Aion V and Hyptec HT were presented for test drives. A dealer conference held in Manila on 27 February brought together 30 core dealers to advance channel-level implementation, marking the first such gathering since the transition to self-operated operations.

GAC International Philippines outlined a 2026 implementation plan across four areas: product, marketing, network and after-sales. The company said its broader localisation mission is framed around integrating into and serving the Philippine market for the long term.
