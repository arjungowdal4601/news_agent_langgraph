Chevrolet has unveiled the 2027 Corvette Grand Sport and Grand Sport X, both powered by the next-generation LS6 6.7L V8 producing 535 horsepower and 520 lb-ft of torque. The naturally aspirated engine, which also replaces the previous unit in the refreshed 2027 Stingray, features a 13.0:1 compression ratio, a 95mm throttle body and a tunnel-ram intake, paired with an eight-speed dual-clutch transmission. The LS6 will be assembled at GM Flint Engine Operations in Michigan, returning V8 production to the city where the first Corvette engines were built in 1955.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/26143515/chevrolet-corvette-grand-sport-2027-1024x739.jpg)


The Grand Sport is a rear-wheel-drive variant offered with three chassis configurations: a standard touring setup on Michelin Pilot Sport All-Season tyres, the Z52 Sport Performance Package with stiffer suspension and Michelin Pilot Sport 4S tyres, and the Z52 Track Performance Package with carbon-ceramic brakes and Michelin Pilot Sport Cup 2R tyres. An available quad centre-exit exhaust marks the first centre-exit option on a pushrod V8 in the eighth-generation Corvette.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/26143942/chevrolet-corvette-grand-sport-2027-s6-6_7l-v8-1024x683.jpg)


The Grand Sport X adds an electric front axle using a 186hp permanent magnetic motor and a 1.9 kWh lithium-ion battery drawn from the Corvette ZR1X, for a combined output of 721hp and all-wheel drive. The system offers three power strategies for track use: Endurance, Qualifying and Push-to-Pass. Electric-only Stealth mode operates up to 50mph, while Shuttle mode allows low-speed movement up to 23mph without engaging the V8.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/26143724/chevrolet-corvette-grand-sport-2027-interior-1024x683.jpg)


Heritage design details include the revival of Admiral Blue Metallic from the C4 era and the repositioning of Grand Sport’s signature hash marks to the rear fenders, reflecting the mid-engine V8’s position behind the cockpit. A Launch Edition interior finished in Santorini Blue with red stitching will be available across multiple exterior colours. Both models will be assembled at GM’s Bowling Green facility.
