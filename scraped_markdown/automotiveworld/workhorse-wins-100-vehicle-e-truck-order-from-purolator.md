Workhorse Group has received a purchase order for 100 fully electric step vans from Canadian logistics provider Purolator, doubling the number of Workhorse vehicles in the operator’s fleet. The order builds on a prior relationship that includes vehicles originally supplied by Motiv Electric Trucks, which merged with Workhorse in late 2025, and deliveries are scheduled across 2026.

In a statement, Scott Griffith, Chief Executive Officer at Workhorse, said: “Purolator has a longstanding commitment to adopting new and innovative technologies to make their fleet more efficient and sustainable, and we are honoured to continue to support them. This is Purolator’s fourth order over a number of years and an important next step in our longstanding partnership.”

Workhorse says it has produced and supported more than 1,100 vehicles to date, which have collectively covered more than 20 million miles. Its manufacturing facility in Union City, Indiana, is capable of producing up to 5,000 vehicles per year on a single operating shift.

The company reports that 10 of the largest medium-duty truck fleets in North America have deployed its vehicles, including Purolator, Vestis and Cintas.
