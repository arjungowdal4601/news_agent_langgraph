Valeo has broken ground on a new manufacturing facility in McAllen, Texas, committing US$225m over five years to produce central compute units for General Motors’ (GM) next-generation electrical architecture. The 337,000 sq ft plant will create up to 500 jobs and begin production in late 2027.

The facility supports one of the largest orders in Valeo’s history and forms part of the company’s “Elevate 2028” strategic plan, which targets growth in software-defined vehicle (SDV) technology. The McAllen plant will produce GM’s central compute unit, a liquid-cooled system that processes data from multiple sensors and systems to manage essential vehicle functions.

In a statement, Kristin Toth, Purchasing Executive Director, Electrical Systems, Software and Connectivity at General Motors, said: “A software-defined vehicle architecture is a key enabler to dramatically improve the customer experience through faster connectivity, richer entertainment options and more frequent updates. Projects such as Valeo’s plant in Texas will play a crucial role as we bring our next-generation electrical architecture to market.”

Jeffrey Shay, Valeo North America President, added: “We’re proud to contribute technologies that enable the next generation of software-defined vehicles with this significant investment in the region and the future of automotive technology. We’re grateful for the opportunity to grow our business with GM in the state of Texas.”
