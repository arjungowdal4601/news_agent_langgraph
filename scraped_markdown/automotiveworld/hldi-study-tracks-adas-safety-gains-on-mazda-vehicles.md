A study by the Highway Loss Data Institute (HLDI) has found that advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) deliver compounding crash reductions as feature bundles grow larger, with newer bundles also incorporating improved versions of core technologies. The research examined Mazda vehicles from model years 2015 to 2023, comparing six ADAS bundles and four stand-alone systems across insurance claim data.

The most basic bundle, comprising front automatic emergency braking (AEB) with forward collision warning, was associated with a 13% reduction in property damage liability (PDL) claim rates and a 9% reduction in bodily injury liability (BIL) claim rates. The most comprehensive bundle, adding pedestrian detection, rear AEB and Driver Attention Alert among its eight features, was linked to a 39% drop in PDL claim rates and a 21% fall in BIL claim rates, though the latter was not statistically significant.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/26083743/iihs-hldi-mazda-1024x576.jpg)


Rear AEB performed strongly by preventing the low-speed parking incidents that make up a large share of insurance claims. In a statement, Matt Moore, Chief Insurance Operations Officer at HLDI and the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety, said: “If you’re worried about rising repair costs, the smartest thing you can do is get a vehicle with rear AEB and make sure it is turned on.”

Blind spot detection and rear cross traffic alert, which share the same rear radar sensors, reduced PDL claim frequency by nearly 10% and BIL claim frequency by 13%. Traffic Sign Recognition showed no clear benefit, with HLDI citing possible system limitations or a small sample size.
