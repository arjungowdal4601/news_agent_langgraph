Daimler Buses has started construction of a new service centre near Rome, replacing its Monterotondo site when it opens in early 2027

Daimler Buses has broken ground on a new service centre in Fiano Romano, around 40 kilometres north of Rome, with the facility scheduled to begin operations in early 2027. The site will handle maintenance and servicing for Mercedes-Benz and Setra buses in the region, replacing the company’s existing centre in Monterotondo.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/03/25084254/Daimler-Buses-service-center-rome-2d-1024x683.jpg)


The new building covers approximately 11,500 square metres, including around 3,000 square metres of covered workshop space. The facility will include 25 bus parking spaces and four dedicated workstations for high-voltage vehicles. The groundbreaking ceremony took place in mid-March 2026 and was attended by representatives from local government and industry.
