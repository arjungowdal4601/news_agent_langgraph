## Mass outage halts Baidu robotaxi fleet across Wuhan roads | Automotive World

Source URL: <https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/mass-outage-halts-baidu-robotaxi-fleet-across-wuhan-roads/>

- More than 100 Apollo Go robotaxis operated by Baidu stalled simultaneously on roads across Wuhan on 31 March.
- Passengers were left stranded in stationary vehicles for up to two hours while customer service systems failed to respond.
- Wuhan police attributed the incident to a system fault that remains under investigation.
- At least three collisions were subsequently reported by other road users encountering the stopped vehicles.
- No fatalities or serious injuries have been reported at the time of writing.
- One driver’s SUV sustained significant front-end damage after the car ahead changed lanes abruptly to avoid a stationary Apollo Go unit.
- The incident sparked widespread discussion on Chinese social media, particularly regarding the safety of autonomous driving technology.
- Dashcam footage shared online showed a car passing 16 immobilised Apollo Go robotaxis in a span of ninety minutes, with drivers braking sharply or changing lanes at the last second to avoid them.
- Baidu’s emergency infrastructure failed during the incident:
  - in-car SOS buttons returned an “unavailable” message,
  - calls through the vehicle screen were automatically disconnected,
  - the official customer service line offered assurances that a technician was en route without one ever actually arriving.
- One passenger on the Third Ring Road elevated highway, speaking to Chinese outlet *DuTe News* while still inside his vehicle, reportedly waited nearly two hours before police and Baidu staff reached him.
- Another passenger complained he was still charged the full fare for the journey.
- Wuhan police later published a statement confirming the scale of the outage and noting that all passengers exited safely.
- The cause has not been formally disclosed, and Baidu has not responded to media requests for comment.
- Baidu customer service attributed the incidents to network issues.
- Apollo Go is among the largest commercial robotaxi services in the world, operating either paid services or testing operations across more than 22 cities worldwide.
- As of February 2026, the company reported 20 million cumulative rides and more than 300 million kilometres of autonomous miles driven.
- The company is rolling out commercial services in both Abu Dhabi and Dubai, and is planning for likewise in Switzerland.
- China remains the company’s bread and butter, and Wuhan has served as one of the company’s most permissive deployment environments.
- Local regulations permit fully driverless vehicles on highways and airport routes.
- Wuhan’s status as a flagship market for the service makes the outage particularly damaging, because it occurred under conditions Baidu has been held up as having mastered.
- Mass fleet stalls are not unique to Apollo Go:
  - in late 2025, a power outage in San Francisco caused dozens of Waymo vehicles to enter a fail-safe mode and stop in intersections, blocking traffic until remote operators intervened,
  - earlier GM Cruise incidents in the same city included vehicles converging on a single intersection after a software path-finding failure and a separate cluster stall linked to network congestion during a large outdoor event.
- High-scrutiny autonomous driving incidents have typically involved vehicles making incorrect decisions at speed.
- Examples include the Xiaomi SU7 crash and the Tesla Autopilot-related crash in Florida.
- The Wuhan outage highlights a different risk profile: a fleet-wide software event that turns every affected unit into a static hazard, in the precise locations where the service had been granted its greatest operational freedom.


## Audi introduces torque vectoring world first in RS 5 | Automotive World

Source URL: <https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/audi-introduces-torque-vectoring-world-first-in-rs-5/>

- Audi has introduced electromechanical torque vectoring at the rear axle of the new RS 5, which it describes as a world first in a production model.
- The system is called **quattro with Dynamic Torque Control**.
- It forms part of a modular plug-in hybrid drivetrain.
- It can distribute torque differences of up to **2,000 Nm** between the rear wheels in **15 milliseconds**.
- The system operates independently of drivetrain load.
- The rear transaxle is purpose-designed for the RS 5’s hybrid drive.
- It houses a water-cooled **400-volt permanent-magnet electric motor** producing **8 kW** and **40 Nm**, acting as a high-voltage actuator.
- Overdrive gears and a conventional differential with a low lock percentage work with the actuator to direct torque to individual rear wheels.
- Unlike clutch-based torque splitters, the system functions when off-throttle and under braking.
- Audi says this gives the system a broader operational envelope than previous approaches.

![](https://media.automotiveworld.com/app/uploads/2026/04/01084947/audi-rs-5-1024x683.jpg)

- Audi says the system is managed by the **HCP1 High-Performance Computing Platform**, the central control unit for drivetrain and suspension functions.
- HCP1 integrates steering input, vehicle dynamics data, and environmental conditions to coordinate torque distribution in real time.
- The RS 5’s electromechanical torque vectoring works with a front-axle electronic differential lock, brake torque vectoring, and twin-valve adaptive shock absorbers calibrated to the rear system.
- The RS 5 is offered across multiple Audi drive select modes, from neutral and balanced to rear-biased configurations.

## NTSB faults Ford’s ADAS tech in two fatal highway crashes | Automotive World

Source URL: <https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/ntsb-faults-fords-adas-tech-in-two-fatal-highway-crashes/>

- The US National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) concluded that Ford’s [BlueCruise](https://www.automotiveworld.com/topics/software-defined-vehicle/ford-to-expand-bluecruise-to-four-additional-models/) advanced driver assistance system (ADAS) failed to mitigate driver distraction in two fatal 2024 crashes.
- NTSB said neither incident would have been avoided even under the system’s updated version.
- The findings were delivered at a three-hour hearing on **31 March 2026**.
- NTSB also criticised the absence of federal performance standards governing partial automation systems and urged regulators to address the gap.
- Both crashes involved **2022 Ford Mustang Mach-E SUVs** operating on BlueCruise on interstate highways.
- One crash occurred in **San Antonio in February 2024**; the other occurred in **Philadelphia one month later**.
- In both cases, the vehicle struck stationary cars at highway speed with no braking recorded from either the driver or the system.
- The crashes resulted in a total of **three fatalities**.
- NTSB found BlueCruise’s driver monitoring failed to detect inattention in both instances.
- NTSB said the system permitted drivers to engage in aggravating driving decisions.
- Specifically, it allowed drivers to disable automatic emergency braking.
- It also permitted speeds up to **20 mph above the posted limit**.
- Ford said it would take the recommendations under serious consideration.
- Ford also noted that one of the two drivers had been impaired by alcohol.
- The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration said it currently has **three open investigations** into BlueCruise and would review the findings.
- NTSB chair **Jennifer Homendy** said: “We cannot take a hands-off approach to hands-free driving technology. Lives depend on it.”
- NTSB said the absence of a federal framework for **SAE Level 2** systems has allowed automakers to set their own parameters for monitoring, speed limits, and data recording.
- NTSB criticised the opacity this arrangement has led to.
- It said the lack of mandatory data recording requirements made critical information difficult to reconstruct.
- NTSB called on the Department of Transportation to establish standardised performance requirements across the industry.
- The findings add to a pattern of NTSB scrutiny of partial automation systems, including a **2023 Tesla Autopilot investigation** that prompted a recall of **two million vehicles**.
- NTSB is also investigating fully autonomous driving operations, including ongoing incidents in US school districts involving Waymo robotaxis [illegally passing](https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/ntsb-waymo-driver-acted-illegally-around-school-buses/) stopped school buses.

- Two scenarios were tested:
  - An Audi vehicle equipped with Ettifos’ V2X-AIR OBU established continuous **PC5-based communication** with Kapsch’s RSUs as the vehicle passed through a tolling zone.

  - An aftermarket, dashboard-mounted configuration of the same OBU processed tolling events and **Traveler Information Messages** in real time via Ettifos’ application interface.

- The **V2X-AIR** unit is **Build America, Buy America (BABA)** compliant, a requirement for devices used in federally funded US infrastructure projects.
- Ettifos CEO **Hojun Kim** said: “This collaboration demonstrates the importance of interoperability in enabling scalable V2X tolling across the ecosystem, highlighting Ettifos’ role in delivering a solution that supports both OEM and aftermarket implementations for immediate deployment.”
