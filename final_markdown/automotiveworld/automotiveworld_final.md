## Audi introduces torque vectoring world first in RS 5 | Automotive World

Source URL: <https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/audi-introduces-torque-vectoring-world-first-in-rs-5/>

- Audi says the new RS 5 gets electromechanical torque vectoring at the rear axle via **quattro with Dynamic Torque Control** — described as a **world first in a production model**.
- The modular plug-in hybrid drivetrain can distribute torque differences of up to **2,000 Nm** between the rear wheels in **15 milliseconds**, operating independently of drivetrain load.
- The purpose-designed rear transaxle houses a **water-cooled 400-volt permanent-magnet electric motor** rated at **8 kW** and **40 Nm**, acting as a high-voltage actuator; overdrive gears and a conventional differential with a low lock percentage work with it to direct torque to individual rear wheels.
- Unlike clutch-based torque splitters, the system works **off-throttle** and **under braking**; Audi says the **HCP1 High-Performance Computing Platform** coordinates torque distribution in real time using steering input, vehicle dynamics data and environmental conditions, alongside a front-axle electronic differential lock, brake torque vectoring and twin-valve adaptive shock absorbers.
- The RS 5 is offered across multiple **Audi drive select** modes, from neutral and balanced to rear-biased configurations.

- The findings add to NTSB scrutiny of partial automation, including a **2023 Tesla Autopilot** investigation that prompted a recall of **two million vehicles**.
- The agency is also investigating fully autonomous driving operations, including ongoing incidents in US school districts involving Waymo robotaxis [illegally passing](https://www.automotiveworld.com/news/ntsb-waymo-driver-acted-illegally-around-school-buses/) stopped school buses.
