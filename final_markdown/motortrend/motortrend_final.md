## Best Tech 2026: The Lucid Gravity Proves That Fast Charging Is About More Than Power

Source URL: <https://www.motortrend.com/features/lucid-gravity-fast-charging-best-tech-2026>

- Lucid’s Grand Touring Gravity was already about 70% complete when Tesla opened Superchargers to competitors in February 2023, forcing a late charging-system rethink.
- The SUV’s 936-volt pack can accept up to 400 kW on a 1,000-volt fast charger, but would have been limited to 50 kW on most Tesla Superchargers’ 500-volt hardware—effectively Leaf-like charging at many sites.

![](https://hips.hearstapps.com/hmg-prod/images/1c57f4d3-06c3-45da-bc22-9ad6500552c5.jpg?w=768&width=768&q=75&format=webp)

- Lucid senior vice president of engineering and digital Emad Dlala said, “Charging convenience is almost the number one factor for EV adoption,” adding, “We can’t underestimate that the Tesla Supercharger network has made Tesla’s success.”
- In weeks, Lucid switched from CCS to a Tesla-style NACS port, moved it to the driver’s rear fender, and replaced the DC-to-DC converter by routing power through the rear motor’s windings to raise 500-volt electricity to charging voltage; peak power at Tesla V3 stations rose to 225 kW, just under their 250 kW maximum.

![](https://hips.hearstapps.com/hmg-prod/images/01f553b3-ce17-4fab-92cb-1afcd59c21e0.jpg?w=768&width=768&q=75&format=webp)

- On the right equipment, the Gravity Grand Touring is the fastest-charging SUV MotorTrend has ever tested: 15 minutes on the plug adds 194 miles, or nearly three hours of driving at 70 mph.
- The 400-kW peak is enabled by nickel-manganese-cobalt lithium-ion cells co-developed with Panasonic, and Lucid says real-world Air owner data let it tune charging software more aggressively without battery damage.

## Volkswagen’s 2027 Atlas Looks New—But Is It Actually Better?

Source URL: <https://www.motortrend.com/news/first-look-2027-volkswagen-atlas-redesign-tech-engine>

- The 2027 Volkswagen Atlas gets all-new sheetmetal and interior plus a new electrical architecture, but it still rides on an evolution of the existing MQB (modular transverse) platform, the same wheelbase, and a lightly massaged EA888 engine.
- VW says the updated electrical architecture is important even though it is not the centralized software-defined system Rivian designed for Volkswagen; it enables a range of new functionality.
- The look is bolder and more premium: a glossy black grille, twin light bars with six daytime running lamps per side, a light-up VW logo on all but the base trim, and animated lighting when approaching or walking away.
- Rear lighting spans a thin LED strip across the car with 15 LED elements per side and another light-up VW; base models ride on 18-inch wheels, while higher trims get 20- or 21-inch wheels.
- VW’s Gen 5 engine, first seen in the 2026 Volkswagen Tiguan, makes 282 hp and 258 lb-ft—13 hp more than last year but 15 lb-ft less—and uses a variable-geometry turbo inlet nozzle, Miller-cycle cam timing, and a more interconnected cooling system to preserve similar performance with slightly better fuel economy on regular gas.
- The new torque delivery uses a quicker ramp to a slightly lower peak that lasts longer; VW says the old fuel-enrichment strategy used for extra torque is no longer allowed.
