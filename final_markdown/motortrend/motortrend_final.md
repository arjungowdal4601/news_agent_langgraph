## Best Tech 2026: The Lucid Gravity Proves That Great Fast-Charging Is About More Than Power

Source URL: <https://www.motortrend.com/features/lucid-gravity-fast-charging-best-tech-2026>

- Lucid had to rethink the [Lucid Gravity](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/lucid/gravity) SUV’s charging strategy after Tesla opened Supercharger access to competitors in February 2023; by then the Grand Touring was already about 70 percent complete, and the team had previously treated Tesla’s network as an afterthought.
- The Gravity’s original 936-volt pack could accept up to 400 kW on a 1,000-volt fast charger, but at Tesla’s nearly 18,000 North American Superchargers — most limited to 500 volts — it would have been capped at 50 kW, effectively giving a six-figure luxury SUV the charging performance of a 10-year-old Nissan Leaf at most Tesla chargers.
- Emad Dlala, Lucid’s senior vice president of engineering and digital, said: “Charging convenience is almost the number one factor for EV adoption,” and “We can’t underestimate that the Tesla Supercharger network has made Tesla’s success.” Lucid then redesigned the system in weeks: swapped CCS for a Tesla-style NACS port, moved it from the driver’s-side front to the driver’s rear fender, and routed power through the rear motor’s windings instead of using a DC-to-DC converter that would have limited charging to 50 kW.
- The result: peak charging power of 225 kW on Tesla V3 stations, just under their 250 kW maximum, and a 2026 MotorTrend Best Tech award in the Public Charging Experience category. On the right equipment, the __Gravity Grand Touring is the fastest charging SUV__*MotorTrend *has ever tested; 15 minutes on the plug adds 194 miles, or nearly three hours of driving at 70 mph.
- Lucid says the 400-kW peak is enabled by nickel-manganese-cobalt lithium-ion cells developed with Panasonic, and that charging software based on real-world use of the [Lucid Air](https://www.motortrend.com/cars/lucid/air) let engineers be more aggressive without damaging the battery.

## Volkswagen’s 2027 Atlas Looks New—But Is It Actually Better?

Source URL: <https://www.motortrend.com/news/first-look-2027-volkswagen-atlas-redesign-tech-engine>

- The 2027 [Volkswagen Atlas](http://www.motortrend.com/cars/volkswagen/atlas) rides on an evolution of the existing MQB (modular transverse) platform, keeps the same wheelbase, and uses a lightly massaged version of the EA888 engine — so it sounds more facelifty than all-new, despite new sheetmetal, a new interior, and a new electrical architecture.
- VW says the updated electrical architecture is important for enabling new functionality, even if it is not the centralized software-defined system Rivian designed for Volkswagen.
- Exterior changes include a more bold, blocky, premium look, a longer nose for pedestrian protection, a glossy black grille, twin light bars with six daytime running lamps per side, a light-up VW logo on all but the base trim, and rear lighting with a thin LED strip plus 15 LED elements per side; the lighting animates when approaching or walking away.
- Base models ride on 18-inch wheels, while higher trims get 20- or 21-inch wheels.
- The Gen 5 engine, first seen in the [2026 Volkswagen Tiguan](http://www.motortrend.com/cars/volkswagen/tiguan), makes 282 hp and 258 lb-ft — the most powerful Atlas yet, up 13 hp but down 15 lb-ft versus last year. VW says a variable-geometry turbo inlet nozzle, new Miller-cycle cam timing, and a more interconnected cooling system should deliver a quicker ramp to a slightly lower torque peak, with the same performance and slightly better fuel efficiency on regular gas.

## Here’s What Hyundai’s Boulder Needs to Fight the Jeep Wrangler and Ford Bronco

Source URL: <https://www.motortrend.com/news/what-2028-hyundai-boulder-needs-fight-rival-jeep-wrangler-ford-bronco>

- The Hyundai Boulder is still a concept, but its reveal shows Hyundai is seriously targeting the U.S. off-road market; instead of a pickup to face the [Ford Ranger](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2024-ford-ranger-supercrew-4x4-xlt-first-test-review), Hyundai chose an SUV aimed squarely at the Jeep Wrangler and Ford Bronco.
- The concept debuted at the 2026 New York International Auto Show, and the article says there’s little doubt Hyundai will turn it into a production model; the challenge is making the body-on-frame SUV thrive against the [Wrangler and Bronco](https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2021-ford-bronco-vs-2021-jeep-wrangler-daily-livability-comparison-review), whose heritage can be both an advantage and a burden.
- For the base model, the writeup wants mechanical simplicity with ruggedness: a naturally aspirated V-6 or turbocharged I-4 with a manual transmission, 35-inch tires as a sensible entry size, and a set of differential lockers that the driver can engage — specifically true mechanical lockers, not ABS-based substitutes.
- It also argues the Boulder should be a true 4WD SUV with a true transfer case rather than AWD, plus a shifter beside the transmission shifter instead of buttons or knobs, to reinforce the mechanical feel off-road drivers want.
